üìù II. Lecture Hall: Audience Response Archive

Q1: When is the best time to take Simvastatin?
Answer: Evening.
Rationale: Cholesterol synthesis peaks at night. Administering at bedtime ensures the drug is at peak concentration when the body is making the most fat.
Q4: Priority interventions for IV Furosemide (Lasix)?
Actions: Check for Tinnitus & Monitor Potassium.
Rationale: Loop diuretics are ototoxic and potassium-wasting. Rapid IV push can cause permanent hearing loss and lethal arrhythmias.
Q10: Who is at highest risk for Digoxin Toxicity?
Answer: Patients on Furosemide (Lasix).
Rationale: Lasix lowers potassium. Hypokalemia makes the heart hypersensitive to Digoxin, precipitating toxicity.
Q22: A Metformin patient is extremely sleepy (Somnolence). Why?
Warning: Sign of Lactic Acidosis.
Rationale: Lactic acidosis is a fatal complication. Somnolence + hyperventilation = medical emergency.
Q24: Mixing Insulin: Which comes first?
Answer: Regular (Clear) before NPH (Cloudy).
Rationale: Prevents the protein in the NPH (cloudy) vial from contaminating the pure regular (clear) vial. Mnemonic: "RN" (Regular then NPH).

üõ† IV. Nursing Interventions Cheat Sheet

1. Digoxin: Check Apical Pulse for 1 Full Minute (Hold < 60).
2. Heparin: Monitor aPTT; Antidote is Protamine Sulfate.
3. Warfarin: Monitor PT/INR; Antidote is Vitamin K.
4. Nitrates: Provide 8-12 hour "Nitrate-Free" window to prevent tolerance.
5. SQ Heparin: 90-degree angle; Do NOT aspirate; Do NOT massage.
