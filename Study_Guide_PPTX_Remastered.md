*Flashcard Style: Focus on the bold logic.*

### ü©∫ HYPERLIPIDEMIA
> **Q1: When is the best time to take Simvastatin?**
> *   **Answer:** **Evening.**
> *   **Rationale:** Cholesterol synthesis peaks at night. Administering at bedtime ensures the drug is at peak concentration when the body is making the most fat.

> **Q2: What lab is needed before starting Atorvastatin?**
> *   **Answer:** **LFTs (Liver Function Tests).**
> *   **Rationale:** Statins are metabolized by the liver and can cause hepatotoxicity. Baseline enzymes are mandatory for safety.

### üíß DIURETICS & BP
> **Q4: Priority interventions for IV Furosemide (Lasix)?**
> *   **Action 1:** Check for **Tinnitus** (sign of ear damage).
> *   **Action 2:** Monitor **Potassium** (risk of life-threatening low K+).
> *   **Action 3:** **Dangle legs** / Move slowly (Orthostatic Hypotension risk).

> **Q6: An older patient on Lasix is confused and weak. Why?**
> *   **Diagnosis:** **Hypokalemia.**
> *   **Instruction:** Increase dietary potassium (bananas, potatoes). Confusion/weakness are hallmark low-potassium signs.

### ü´Ä ANGINA & HEART FAILURE
> **Q10: Who is at highest risk for Digoxin Toxicity?**
> *   **Answer:** **Patients on Furosemide (Lasix).**
> *   **Rationale:** Lasix lowers potassium. **Low potassium makes the heart hypersensitive to Digoxin**, which triggers toxicity.

> **Q14: Chest pain is NOT relieved 5 mins after the 1st Nitroglycerin tab. Action?**
> *   **Answer:** **Call 911 immediately.**
> *   **Rationale:** Standard emergency protocol. Do not wait for the 3rd dose if pain doesn't budge after the first.

---

### üç≠ DIABETES HALL
> **Q18: How should Metformin XR be taken?**
> *   **Answer:** **With a meal.**
> *   **Rationale:** Metformin is notorious for GI upset (nausea/diarrhea). Food buffers the stomach.

> **Q22: A Metformin patient is extremely sleepy (Somnolence). Why is this an emergency?**
> *   **Answer:** Sign of **Lactic Acidosis.**
> *   **Rationale:** Rare but fatal. Somnolence combined with hyperventilation indicates toxic acid buildup.

> **Q24: Mixing Insulin: Which comes first?**
> *   **Answer:** **Regular (Clear) before NPH (Cloudy).**
> *   **Mnemonic:** "Clear before Cloudy" or "RN" (Regular to NPH).

### üö´ ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS

*   **Beta Blockers (Non-selective):** **ASTHMA** (Causes bronchospasm).
*   **Pioglitazone (TZDs):** **HEART FAILURE** (Causes severe fluid retention).
*   **Nitroglycerin:** **SILDENAFIL (VIAGRA)** (Fatal BP drop).

## üõ† IV. NURSING INTERVENTIONS CHEAT SHEET
1.  **Digoxin:** Check Apical Pulse for **1 Full Minute** (Hold < 60).
2.  **Heparin:** Monitor **aPTT**; Antidote is **Protamine Sulfate**.
3.  **Warfarin:** Monitor **PT/INR**; Antidote is **Vitamin K**.
4.  **Nitrates:** Provide **8-12 hour "Nitrate-Free" window** to prevent tolerance.
5.  **SQ Heparin:** **90-degree angle**; Do NOT aspirate; Do NOT massage.
