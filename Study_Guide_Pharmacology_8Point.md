## I. HYPERLIPIDEMIA MEDICATIONS

### 1. STATINS (HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors)
1. **Drug Name:** Atorvastatin (Lipitor), Simvastatin (Zocor), Lovastatin (Mevacor), Pravastatin (Pravachol), Rosuvastatin (Crestor), Fluvastatin (Lescol).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Inhibits HMG-CoA reductase (cholesterol synthesis enzyme). Increases LDL uptake.
3. **Indications:** Primary hyperlipidemia, CVD prevention.
4. **Contraindications:** **Active liver disease**, Pregnancy.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Myalgia**, **Rhabdomyolysis** (tea-colored urine), Hepatotoxicity.
6. **Drug Interactions:** **Grapefruit juice**, Warfarin.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral. **Take in the evening.**
8. **Patient Education:** Report muscle pain immediately. Regular LFT labs needed.

### 2. CHOLESTEROL ABSORPTION INHIBITOR
1. **Drug Name:** Ezetimibe (Zetia).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Blocks cholesterol absorption in the small intestine.
3. **Indications:** Primary hypercholesterolemia.
4. **Contraindications:** Liver disease (if used with statins).
5. **Adverse Effects:** GI upset, fatigue, joint pain.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Statins (liver risk), Bile acid sequestrants (separate by 2-4 hrs).
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral, 10mg daily.
8. **Patient Education:** Follow low-cholesterol diet.

### 3. BILE ACID RESINS
1. **Drug Name:** Cholestyramine (Questran), Colesevelam (Welchol).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Binds bile acids in intestine.
3. **Indications:** Hyperlipidemia.
4. **Contraindications:** Biliary obstruction.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Constipation**, bloating.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Binds most meds. **Take other meds 1h before or 4-6h after.**
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Powder mixed with 2-6 oz liquid.
8. **Patient Education:** Increase fiber/fluids.

### 4. FIBRIC ACIDS (Fibrates)
1. **Drug Name:** Gemfibrozil (Lopid), Fenofibrate (Tricor).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Lowers triglycerides, raises HDL.
3. **Indications:** Severe hypertriglyceridemia.
4. **Contraindications:** Gallbladder disease.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Gallstones**, Hepatotoxicity.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Warfarin, Statins (rhabdo risk).
7. **Dosage & Administration:** **Take 30 min before breakfast/dinner.**
8. **Patient Education:** Report RUQ pain.

### 5. NIACIN (Vitamin B3)
1. **Drug Name:** Niacin (Nicotinic Acid).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Limits fat production in liver.
3. **Indications:** High triglycerides.
4. **Contraindications:** Liver disease, gout.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Intense flushing/itching**, hypotension.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Antihypertensives.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral. 1-6g/day.
8. **Patient Education:** **Take Aspirin 30 min before** to reduce flushing.

## II. ANTIHYPERTENSIVE MEDICATIONS

### 6. ACE INHIBITORS ("-pril")
1. **Drug Name:** Lisinopril, Enalapril, Captopril.
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Blocks Angiotensin I -> II. Vasodilation.
3. **Indications:** HTN, HF, diabetic nephropathy.
4. **Contraindications:** **Pregnancy**, History of angioedema.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Dry cough**, **Angioedema** (emergency), **Hyperkalemia**.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Potassium supplements, NSAIDs.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral. **Captopril: Take on empty stomach.**
8. **Patient Education:** Report facial swelling. Avoid salt substitutes.

### 7. ARBs ("-sartan")
1. **Drug Name:** Losartan, Valsartan.
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Blocks Angiotensin II receptors.
3. **Indications:** HTN, HF.
4. **Contraindications:** Pregnancy.
5. **Adverse Effects:** Dizziness, hyperkalemia. No cough.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Same as ACE-I.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral.
8. **Patient Education:** Notify if pregnant.

### 8. CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKERS (CCBs)
1. **Drug Name:** Amlodipine (DHP), Verapamil/Diltiazem (Non-DHP).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Blocks Ca entry into smooth muscle.
3. **Indications:** HTN, Angina, Dysrhythmias.
4. **Contraindications:** Heart block. Non-DHPs contraindicated in HF.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Peripheral edema** (-dipines), **Constipation** (Verapamil), Bradycardia.
6. **Drug Interactions:** **Grapefruit juice**, Beta-blockers (conduction risk).
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral/IV. Take between meals.
8. **Patient Education:** Avoid grapefruit juice. Check pulse.

### 9. BETA BLOCKERS ("-olol")
1. **Drug Name:** Metoprolol (Selective), Propranolol (Non-selective).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Blocks beta receptors. Reduces HR/CO.
3. **Indications:** HTN, HF, MI, Angina.
4. **Contraindications:** **Asthma (Non-selective)**, Bradycardia.
5. **Adverse Effects:** Bradycardia, Fatigue. **Masks hypoglycemia symptoms.**
6. **Drug Interactions:** CCBs, Insulin.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral/IV. **Apical pulse check (hold if <60).**
8. **Patient Education:** **Do NOT stop abruptly.** Diabetics monitor BG.

### 10. DIURETICS
1. **Drug Name:** Furosemide (Loop), HCTZ (Thiazide), Spironolactone (K-Sparing).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Loop/Thiazide: Excrete K. K-Sparing: Retain K.
3. **Indications:** HTN, HF, Edema.
4. **Contraindications:** Renal failure (Thiazide), Hyperkalemia (K-Sparing).
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Ototoxicity** (Loop), **Hypokalemia** (Loop/Thiazide), **Hyperkalemia** (K-Sparing).
6. **Drug Interactions:** Digoxin (toxicity with low K+), Lithium (toxicity with low Na+).
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral/IV. **Take in the morning.**
8. **Patient Education:** Weigh daily. Loop/Thiazide: Eat high K+. K-Sparing: Avoid high K+.

## III. ANGINA & HEART FAILURE

### 11. NITRATES
1. **Drug Name:** Nitroglycerin, Isosorbide Dinitrate.
2. **Class/Mechanism:** Vasodilation; reduces preload.
3. **Indications:** Angina.
4. **Contraindications:** **Sildenafil (Viagra)**.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Headache**, Hypotension.
6. **Drug Interactions:** Alcohol, PDE5 inhibitors.
7. **Dosage & Administration:** SL: q5min x 3. Patch: **8-12h nitrate-free period required.**
8. **Patient Education:** Nitrate-free period prevents tolerance. SL stinging is normal.

### 12. CARDIAC GLYCOSIDE: DIGOXIN
1. **Drug Name:** Digoxin (Lanoxin).
2. **Class/Mechanism:** (+) Inotropic, (-) Chronotropic.
3. **Indications:** HF, AFib.
4. **Contraindications:** heart block.
5. **Adverse Effects:** **Toxicity (Yellow halos, N/V, Bradycardia).**
6. **Drug Interactions:** **Loop Diuretics (Low K+ increases toxicity risk).**
7. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral/IV. **Check apical pulse 1 min (hold if <60).**
8. **Patient Education:** Report vision changes. Antidote: Digibind.

## IV. DIABETES MEDICATIONS

### 13. METFORMIN
1. **Class/Mechanism:** Biguanide; reduces liver glucose production.
2. **Indications:** Type 2 DM (1st line).
3. **Contraindications:** Renal impairment (Cr >1.5).
4. **Adverse Effects:** **Lactic Acidosis** (emergency), GI upset, B12 deficiency.
5. **Drug Interactions:** **Iodine Contrast (Hold 48h before/after).**
6. **Dosage & Administration:** Oral, take with meals.
7. **Patient Education:** Avoid alcohol. Report extreme fatigue.

### 14. SULFONYLUREAS (Glipizide)
1. **Class/Mechanism:** Stimulates pancreas to release insulin.
2. **Indications:** Type 2 DM.
3. **Contraindications:** Type 1 DM.
4. **Adverse Effects:** **Hypoglycemia**, Weight gain.
5. **Drug Interactions:** Alcohol, Beta-blockers.
6. **Dosage & Administration:** **30 min before meals.**
7. **Patient Education:** Skip meal = skip dose. Carry glucose.

### 15. INSULINS
1. **Types:** Lispro (Rapid), Regular (Short), NPH (Intermediate), Glargine (Long).
2. **Mechanism:** Promotes glucose uptake.
3. **Indications:** Type 1 & 2 DM.
4. **Adverse Effects:** **Hypoglycemia**, **Lipohypertrophy**.
5. **Drug Interactions:** Beta-blockers.
6. **Dosage & Administration:** SC. **Regular is the ONLY one for IV.** **Clear (Regular) before Cloudy (NPH).**
7. **Patient Education:** Rotate sites. 15/15 Rule for hypoglycemia.

## V. LECTURE SLIDE PRACTICE QUESTIONS (The "Audience Response" Items)

### HYPERLIPIDEMIA
*   **Q:** Information to include when teaching about Simvastatin?
    *   **A:** **Take this medication in the evening.** (Rationale: Cholesterol synthesis is highest at night).
*   **Q:** Baseline procedure required prior to starting Atorvastatin?
    *   **A:** **Liver function tests (LFTs).** (Rationale: Statins are hepatotoxic).
*   **Q:** Adverse effect of Gemfibrozil to identify during data collection?
    *   **A:** **Diarrhea.** (Rationale: GI disturbances are common).

### DIURETICS & BP
*   **Q:** Interventions for a client receiving Furosemide IV? (Select All)
    *   **A:** **Check for tinnitus; Monitor blood potassium levels; Elevate head of bed slowly before ambulation.** (Rationales: Ototoxicity, hypokalemia, and orthostatic hypotension).
*   **Q:** Finding to report for a client receiving Spironolactone?
    *   **A:** **Blood potassium 5.2 mEq/L.** (Rationale: Hyperkalemia risk).
*   **Q:** Finding to monitor for a client on Torsemide?
    *   **A:** **Hypokalemia, Hypotension, Ototoxicity, Ventricular dysrhythmias.**
*   **Q:** Patient teaching for an older patient on Furosemide with muscle weakness/confusion?
    *   **A:** **Increase potassium in the diet.** (Rationale: These are signs of hypokalemia).

### ACE-I & BETA BLOCKERS
*   **Q:** Instructions for a young adult female on Lisinopril? (Select All)
    *   **A:** **"Report persistent dry cough"; "Monitor BP regularly"; "Notify doctor immediately if you become pregnant."** (Rationale: Teratogenic).
*   **Q:** Contraindication for Propranolol?
    *   **A:** **Asthma.** (Rationale: Non-selective beta-blockade causes bronchospasm).
*   **Q:** Adverse effect to monitor for a client on Captopril?
    *   **A:** **Hyperkalemia.**

### ANGINA & HEART FAILURE
*   **Q:** Appropriate response to a client who has a headache after sublingual Nitroglycerin?
    *   **A:** **"A headache is a common adverse effect... it will probably occur less often over time."**
*   **Q:** Action for unrelieved chest pain 5 min after 1st Nitroglycerin tab?
    *   **A:** **Instruct the client to call 911.** (Then take the second tab).
*   **Q:** Teaching for a client on Verapamil for angina?
    *   **A:** **Monitor for decreased heart rate.** (Rationale: Non-DHP CCB causes bradycardia).
*   **Q:** Adverse effects of Digoxin toxicity to report? (Select All)
    *   **A:** **Fatigue, anorexia, blurred vision.** (Also: nausea, halos).
*   **Q:** Which client is at highest risk for Digoxin toxicity?
    *   **A:** **A client who takes Furosemide for hypertension.** (Rationale: Loop diuretics cause hypokalemia, which potentates Digoxin toxicity).

### DIABETES
*   **Q:** Teaching for Metformin XR?
    *   **A:** **"Take the medication with a meal."** (Rationale: Reduces GI upset).
*   **Q:** Education regarding Glipizide mechanism?
    *   **A:** **Glipizide stimulates the pancreas to release adequate insulin.**
*   **Q:** Instruction for starting Repaglinide?
    *   **A:** **"I'll take this medication 30 minutes before I eat."**
*   **Q:** Lab test to monitor for Acarbose?
    *   **A:** **LFTs.** (Rationale: Can be hepatotoxic with long-term use).
*   **Q:** Adverse effect of Metformin to report?
    *   **A:** **Somnolence.** (Rationale: A sign of lactic acidosis).
*   **Q:** Medication contraindicated with Metformin due to glucose effects?
    *   **A:** **Prednisone.** (Rationale: Steroids cause hyperglycemia).
*   **Q:** Instruction for self-administering Regular and NPH in one syringe?
    *   **A:** **Draw up the regular insulin first.** (Clear before Cloudy).
*   **Q:** Reason to rotate insulin injection sites?
    *   **A:** **To prevent lipohypertrophy.**
*   **Q:** Type of insulin for intravenous (IV) use?
    *   **A:** **Regular insulin (Humulin R).**
*   **Q:** Insulin to treat Ketoacidosis (confused, flushed, sweet breath)?
    *   **A:** **Regular insulin.**
