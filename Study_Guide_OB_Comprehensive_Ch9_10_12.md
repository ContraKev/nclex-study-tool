## Chapter 9: The Family After Birth (Postpartum & Newborn Care)

### 1. Puerperium (Postpartum Period)

The **puerperium** is the 6-week period after birth when the mother's body returns to its pre-pregnant state. Key postpartum factors:

- Maternal/child pair vital sign stability
- Control of postpartum bleeding to WNL
- Establishment of newborn feeding

### 2. BUBBLE Assessment

Postpartum assessment follows the **BUBBLE** acronym:

| Letter | Assessment | Key Findings |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| **B** | Breasts | Soft or engorged; check nipples for redness/cracking |
| **U** | Uterus | Firmness, fundal height, location (1-2 cm/day involution) |
| **B** | Bladder | Distention, voiding ability |
| **B** | Bowels | Gas passage, bowel sounds, stool |
| **L** | Lochia | Character, amount, color, odor, clots |
| **E** | Episiotomy/Perineum | REEDA: Redness, Edema, Ecchymosis, Discharge, Approximation |

**Additional E**: Emotions/bonding with newborn

### 3. Vital Signs Postpartum

- **Pulse**: Some bradycardia is common (50-70 bpm) due to decreased blood volume
- **Respirations**: 12-20/min, should be stable
- **Blood Pressure**: Should be stable; hypotension may indicate hemorrhage
- **Temperature**: Slight elevation first 24 hrs is common; >100.4°F after 24 hrs = infection

### 4. Uterine Involution

**Involution** = return of uterus to non-pregnant state

- Uterus reduces **1-2 cm per day**
- By **10 days**: no longer palpable
- By **5-6 weeks**: returns to pre-pregnant size
- **Fundal height**: Immediately after birth at umbilicus; descends ~1 fingerbreadth/day

**Massage the fundus** if boggy to stimulate contraction and prevent hemorrhage.

### 5. Lochia (Vaginal Discharge)

| Type | Color/Character | Duration |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| **Rubra** | Red, mostly blood | Up to 3 days |
| **Serosa** | Pinkish, blood/mucus mix | Up to 10th day |
| **Alba** | White/yellowish, mostly mucus | 10th-21st day |

> ⚠️ Assess pads AND area under patient (underbed/chux) — blood can overflow beneath. Constant trickle of bright red lochia with firm fundus suggests laceration bleeding.

### 6. Perineum & Vaginal Changes

- **Rugae** (vaginal folds) disappear during birth; reappear by 3 weeks
- Vagina never fully returns to pre-pregnant size
- **Perineum**: Edematous, tender, bruised
- **REEDA assessment** for perineal healing

**Comfort Measures**:
- **First 12-24 hrs**: Ice pack to perineum
- **After 24 hrs**: Sitz bath (increases circulation, promotes healing)
- Perineal care (peri bottle with warm water, front to back)

### 7. Cesarean Section Adaptations

Same as vaginal delivery PLUS:
- Monitor abdominal dressing/incision
- Lochia generally less
- Urinary catheter care
- Respiratory care (incentive spirometer)
- Prevention of thrombophlebitis (SCDs, early ambulation)
- Pain management (PCA, opioids)

### 8. Breastfeeding & Lactation

**Hormones**:
- **Prolactin** (anterior pituitary): Milk production
- **Oxytocin** (posterior pituitary): "Let-down reflex" (milk ejection) — tingling in breasts, uterine cramping

**Contraindications to Breastfeeding**: HIV, HTLV-1 & 2 (transmitted via breast milk)

**Milk Stages**:
| Stage | Timing | Characteristics |
|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **Colostrum** | First few days | Yellowish, thicker; laxative effect; rich in immunoglobulins |
| **Transitional** | 7-10 days | Increased lactose, fewer immunoglobulins |
| **Mature** | By 14 days | Bluish; 20 kcal/oz; all needed nutrients |

**Foremilk vs. Hindmilk**:
- **Foremilk**: First milk; more watery, thirst-quenching
- **Hindmilk**: Later milk; higher fat content; satisfies hunger

**Feeding Schedule**: On demand, every 2-3 hrs for 15-30 min → 8-10 wet diapers/day + ≥1 stool

**Galactagogues** (increase milk supply): Oatmeal, fenugreek, flaxseed, brewer's yeast

**Storage**:
- Room temp: 3 hrs
- Refrigerator: 3 days
- Freezer: 3 months

### 9. Newborn Transition & Care (Ch. 9 Overview)

**"Golden Hour"**: First hour after birth — baby stays with parents if ABCs stable; promotes bonding

**Thermoregulation** (4 mechanisms of heat loss):
| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Evaporation** | Wet skin → dry |
| **Conduction** | Contact with cold surface |
| **Convection** | Drafts/air movement |
| **Radiation** | Heat to cooler objects nearby |

**Interventions**: Dry immediately, skin-to-skin, radiant warmer, hat, warm blankets

### 10. Newborn Hypoglycemia

**Definition**: Blood glucose <45 mg/dL in term infant

**Risk Factors**:
- Preterm or postterm
- Diabetic mother
- LGA or SGA
- IUGR
- Asphyxia
- Cold stress
- Tocolytics during labor

**Signs/Symptoms**:
- Jitteriness, poor muscle tone
- Sweating, respiratory difficulty
- Low temperature, poor suck
- High-pitched cry, lethargy, seizures

### 11. Cord Care

- **Delayed clamping** (30-60 sec) → increased iron stores
- Keep clean and dry
- Fasten diaper below cord
- Report: redness, moist, foul-smelling
- Sponge bath until cord falls off (~1-2 weeks)

### 12. Newborn Safety

- **ID bands**: Match every time baby returned to parent
- Place baby on **far side of bed** (away from door)
- **Car seat**: Rear-facing until age 2; back seat; snug harness
- **Never prop bottle** → choking, ear infections, tooth decay risk

### 13. Discharge Teaching

**Maternal Self-Care**:
- Hygiene (peri care, front to back)
- Sexual intercourse: Safe when bleeding stops + perineum healed (~6 weeks); use lubricant if breastfeeding
- Diet & exercise
- Danger signs to report

**Newborn Discharge Care**:
- Feeding cues
- Safety (car seat, sleep position)
- Follow-up appointments
- Reassure: hospital staff available by phone

## Chapter 10: Nursing Care of Women with Complications After Birth

### 1. Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)

**Definition**:
- Vaginal birth: >500 mL blood loss
- C-section: >1000 mL blood loss

**Timing**:
- **Early (Primary)**: Within 24 hrs
- **Late (Secondary)**: 24 hrs to 6 weeks

**Leading cause of maternal mortality worldwide**

### 2. Signs of Hypovolemic Shock

> ⚠️ **Tachycardia is usually the FIRST sign** (before BP drops)

| Sign | Finding |
|------|---------|
| Pulse | Tachycardia (rapid) |
| BP | Falling systolic |
| Skin | Pale, cold, clammy |
| Mental status | Changes, confusion |
| Urine output | Decreased |

### 3. The 4 T's of PPH

| Cause | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Tone** | Uterine atony (most common cause) |
| **Trauma** | Lacerations, hematomas |
| **Tissue** | Retained placental fragments |
| **Thrombin** | Coagulopathy (DIC, eclampsia) |

### 4. Uterine Atony

**Definition**: Lack of normal muscle tone — uterus is "boggy" or soft

**Assessment**:
- Fundus difficult to feel
- High fundal height (above umbilicus)
- Excessive bleeding

**Risk Factors**:
- Overdistended uterus (polyhydramnios, large baby, multiple babies)
- Prolonged labor, precipitous labor
- Retained placenta
- Full bladder
- Grand multiparity
- Precipitous delivery

**Key Nursing Pearl**: Fundus deviated to **right** = **bladder distention** → assist to void FIRST before massage

**First Action for Abnormal Bleeding**: Assess the fundus! (Always check tone before treating)

**Treatment**:
- Fundal massage
- Empty bladder
- Medications: Oxytocin, methylergonovine (Methergine), prostaglandins (Hemabate, Misoprostol)

**Methylergonovine (Methergine)**: Contraindicated in hypertension (causes severe vasoconstriction)

### 5. Lacerations vs. Atony

| Finding | Atony | Laceration |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Uterus | Boggy, hard to locate | Firm |
| Bleeding | Dark red, with clots | **Bright red**, continuous trickle |
| Treatment | Massage, oxytocin | Surgical repair |

### 6. Hematomas

**Causes**: Trauma during delivery (forceps, vacuum, episiotomy)

**Signs**: Severe perineal pain, swelling, discoloration; may have difficulty voiding

**Treatment**: Ice, analgesics, possible surgical evacuation if large

### 7. Subinvolution

**Definition**: Slower than expected return of uterus to non-pregnant state

**Signs**:
- Fundal height higher than expected
- Persistent lochia rubra
- Pelvic pain, heaviness, fatigue

**Treatment**: Methergine, antibiotics, D&C if retained tissue

### 8. Thromboembolic Disorders

**Risk Factors** (Virchow's Triad):
- Venous stasis (pregnancy)
- Hypercoagulability (↑ fibrinogen, ↓ fibrinolysis)
- Endothelial injury (delivery trauma)

**Types**:
| Type | Location | Key Findings |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **SVT** | Superficial veins (saphenous) | Localized redness, tenderness, warmth |
| **DVT** | Deep veins (femoral, popliteal) | Leg circumference >2 cm difference, edema, pain, Homan's sign |
| **PE** | Pulmonary artery | Sudden chest pain, dyspnea, cough, ↓ LOC, hemoptysis |

**Prevention**:
- Early ambulation
- SCDs (sequential compression devices)
- Avoid pressure behind knees (popliteal space)
- Anti-embolic stockings

**Treatment**:
- SVT: Analgesics, heat, elevation
- DVT: Heparin or LMWH (Lovenox), elevation
- PE: ICU transfer, anticoagulation

**Anticoagulant Teaching**:
- Soft-bristled toothbrush (gum bleeding = excess anticoagulation)
- Report: nosebleeds, unexplained bruising, prolonged bleeding
- Vitamin K = antidote for warfarin overdose
- LMWH contraindicated with regional anesthesia

### 9. Puerperal Infection (Postpartum Sepsis)

**Definition**: Fever ≥38°C (100.4°F) within first 24 hrs AND for ≥2 days during first 10 days postpartum

**Risk Factors**:
- Cracked nipples
- Surgical incision (C-section, episiotomy)
- Tissue trauma during labor
- Retained placenta or blood clots
- ↑ Vaginal pH after birth

**Most common cause**: **Endometritis** (inflammation of uterine lining)

**Prevention**:
- Hand hygiene (primary means)
- Front-to-back peripad application
- Complete antibiotics as prescribed
- Adequate rest and nutrition

**Dangers**: Infection can ascend → uterus → fallopian tubes → peritonitis (life-threatening)

### 10. Mastitis

**Definition**: Infection of breast tissue (usually Staphylococcus aureus)

**Signs**:
- Unilateral breast redness, swelling, warmth, pain
- Flu-like symptoms (fever, chills, body aches)

**Treatment** (5 cornerstones):
1. **Antibiotics** (dicloxacillin, cephalexin)
2. **Milk removal** (continue breastfeeding or pumping — DO NOT stop)
3. **Analgesics** prn
4. **Heat before feeding** (warm compresses, shower)
5. **Cold after feeding** (ice pack)

> ⚠️ Weaning during mastitis worsens symptoms (engorgement/milk stasis)

### 11. Postpartum Mood Disorders

| Disorder | Onset | Duration | Characteristics |
|----------|-------|----------|-----------------|
| **Postpartum Blues (Baby Blues)** | Days 2-3 | Resolves by day 10 | Tearfulness, mood swings, anxiety — NORMAL, self-limiting |
| **Postpartum Depression (PPD)** | Within 2-4 weeks | Weeks to months | Persistent sadness, lack of interest, guilt, sleep/appetite disturbance, inability to cope |
| **Postpartum Psychosis** | Within 2 weeks | Medical emergency | Delusions, hallucinations, confusion, thoughts of harming self/baby |

**Risk Factors for PPD**:
- Inadequate social support
- Poor relationship with partner
- Life/childcare stress
- Low self-esteem
- Unplanned pregnancy
- History of depression

**Nursing Tip**: Never assume "baby blues" — assess for persistence and pervasiveness. Ask about suicidal/homicidal thoughts.

**Treatment**:
- Blues: Support, rest, reassurance
- PPD: Psychotherapy, antidepressants (SSRIs safe in breastfeeding)
- Psychosis: **Immediate psychiatric intervention**, antipsychotics, possible hospitalization

## Chapter 12: The Term Newborn

### 1. Adjustment to Extrauterine Life

Newborn adjustment depends on:
- Genetic background
- Health of uterine environment
- Safe delivery
- Care during first month

### 2. Apgar Score ⭐

Standardized evaluation at **1 and 5 minutes** after birth

| Sign | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|------|---|---|---|
| **Heart Rate** | Absent | <100 bpm | >100 bpm |
| **Respirations** | Absent | Slow, irregular | Good, crying |
| **Muscle Tone** | Limp | Some flexion | Active motion |
| **Reflexes** | No response | Grimace | Cry, cough, sneeze |
| **Color** | Blue/pale | Acrocyanosis | Completely pink |

**Score Interpretation**:
- 7-10: Normal
- 4-6: Moderate distress → assist
- 0-3: Severe distress → immediate resuscitation

### 3. Newborn Vital Signs

| Parameter | Normal Range |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Temperature** | 36.2-37.7°C (97.1-99.8°F) |
| **Heart Rate** | 110-160 bpm (may be higher first 30 min) |
| **Respirations** | 30-60/min |
| **Blood Pressure** | 60-80/40-50 mmHg |

**Acrocyanosis** (blue hands/feet) is normal due to sluggish peripheral circulation — NOT central cyanosis

### 4. Newborn Assessment

**Head Circumference**: 32-36 cm (12.5-14.1 in); should not be >2 cm larger than chest

**Fontanelles**:
- **Anterior**: Diamond-shaped; closes ~18 months
- **Posterior**: Triangular; closes ~2-3 months
- **Flat** = normal; **Bulging** = ↑ ICP; **Depressed** = dehydration

**Birth Injuries**:
| Condition | Description | Crosses Suture Lines? |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Molding** | Overlapping skull bones from birth canal | N/A |
| **Caput Succedaneum** | Swelling of scalp soft tissues | **Yes** |
| **Cephalohematoma** | Bleeding beneath periosteum | **No** |

> Caput resolves in days; cephalohematoma may take weeks

### 5. Newborn Reflexes

| Reflex | Description | Disappears |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Rooting** | Turns head toward touch on cheek | 3-4 months |
| **Sucking** | Sucks when nipple/pacifier touches lips | 4 months (voluntary) |
| **Moro (Startle)** | Arms extend then adduct when startled | 4-6 months |
| **Grasp (Palmar)** | Fingers curl around object in palm | 3-4 months |
| **Babinski** | Toes fan out when sole stroked | 1 year |
| **Stepping** | "Walking" motions when held upright | 2 months |
| **Tonic Neck (Fencing)** | Head turned → arm extends on that side | 4-6 months |

### 6. Pain Assessment in Newborns

Use validated scales:
- **CRIES**: Crying, Requires O₂, ↑ vital signs, Expression, Sleeplessness (10 pt)
- **FLACC**: Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability (10 pt) — commonly used
- **NIPS**: Neonatal Infant Pain Scale
- **NPASS**: Neonatal Pain, Agitation, Sedation Scale (most common)

### 7. Thermoregulation

Newborn cannot regulate temperature well:
- **Sweat glands don't function** → risk of overheating
- Cold stress → ↑ O₂ consumption, ↓ glucose, acidosis

**Maintain**:
- Dry immediately after birth
- Skin-to-skin contact
- Radiant warmer
- Hat, warm blankets

### 8. Newborn Nutrition

**Meconium**: First stool (black, tarry) — passed within 24-48 hrs
**Transitional stool**: Greenish-brown
**Breastfed stool**: Yellow, seedy, soft
**Formula-fed stool**: Pale yellow/tan, firmer

**Feeding increases gradually** with growth

**Vitamins**:
- **Vitamin D**: Commonly prescribed (breastfed infants at risk for deficiency)
- Vitamin K: Given IM at birth to prevent hemorrhagic disease

### 9. Infection Prevention

Newborn immune system is immature:
- **Hand hygiene** = primary prevention
- Umbilical cord stump = primary infection site if not kept clean
- Avoid sick contacts
- Proper handwashing before handling

### 10. Discharge Planning & Parent Teaching

**Key Teaching Topics**:
- Feeding cues and techniques
- Sleep safety (back to sleep, firm mattress)
- Cord care
- Circumcision care (if applicable)
- Bathing (sponge until cord falls off)
- Car seat safety
- Signs of illness to report
- Follow-up pediatrician appointment (within 48-72 hrs if discharged <48 hrs)

**Infant Abduction Prevention**:
- ID bands checked every time
- Never leave baby alone in room
- Educate parents about security

## Quick Reference: Danger Signs to Report

### Mother
- Heavy bleeding (saturating >1 pad/hr)
- Foul-smelling lochia
- Fever >100.4°F
- Severe headache, vision changes (preeclampsia signs)
- Chest pain, shortness of breath
- Leg swelling/redness/pain (DVT)
- Feelings of harming self or baby

### Newborn
- Temperature <97°F or >100.4°F
- Difficulty breathing, grunting, retractions
- Cyanosis (central)
- Seizures, jitteriness
- Poor feeding, lethargy
- No wet diaper in 12 hrs or no stool in 48 hrs
- Yellow skin (jaundice) in first 24 hrs
- Cord redness, foul odor, bleeding
