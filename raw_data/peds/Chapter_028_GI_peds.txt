Congenital Disorders Esophageal atresia (tracheoesophageal fistula [TEF])  TEF is caused by a failure of the tissues of the GI tract to separate properly in prenatal life. Four types Upper and lower esophagus (from the stomach) end in a blind pouch. Upper esophagus ends in a blind pouch; lower esophagus (from stomach) connects to the trachea. Upper esophagus is attached to trachea; lower esophagus (from stomach) also attached to trachea. Upper esophagus connects to trachea; lower esophagus (from stomach) ends in a blind pouch. Pyloric Stenosis Obstruction of the lower end of the stomach caused by overgrowth of the circular muscles of the pylorus or spasms of the sphincter Commonly classified as a congenital anomaly Symptoms usually do not appear until an infant is 2 or 3 weeks old. Most common surgical condition of GI tract in infancy Incidence is higher in boys. Copyright © 2019, Elsevier Inc. All rights reserved. 7
