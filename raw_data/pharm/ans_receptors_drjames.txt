Adrenergic neurotransmitters and receptors  
❖ Epinephrine  and norepinephrine  activate adrenergic receptors  of the sympathetic system .  
Medications that act like  epinephrine and norepinephrine are “ sympathomimetic .” 
❖ A medication that blocks adrenergic receptors  will block the  action of the  sympathetic 
system .  Medications that do this are “ sympatholytic .” 
❖ There are 4 main classes of adrenergic receptors:  apha -1, alpha -2, beta -1, and beta -2.

 Cholinergic neurotransmitters and receptors  
❖ Acetylcholine  is a neurotransmitter that activates muscarinic receptors  of the 
parasympathetic system .  Medications that do this are “ cholinergic .” 
❖ A medication that blocks , or has the effect of blocking, the muscarinic receptors  will block 
the parasympathetic system and its symptoms.  Medications that do this are 
“anticholinergic .”  Many medications have anticholinergic side effects and this is often 
tested on the NCLEX.

muscarinic receptors.
