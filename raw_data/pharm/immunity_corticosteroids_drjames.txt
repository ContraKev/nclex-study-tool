=== SLIDE 10 ===
Side Effects of Glucocorticoids
Side effects with glucocorticoids vary depending upon which route of administration is required. Oral or systemic administration has greater side effect potential.

Immunosuppression – risk of infection
Steroid-induced diabetes – hyperglycemia
Bone effects – bone loss and osteoporosis
GI effects – dyspepsia, peptic ulcer
Muscle weakness 
Mood alterations – depression, psychosis, confusion, insomnia
Mineralocorticoid effects – hypertension, fluid retention, edema and hypokalemia
Increased appetite and weight gain
Increased risk of heart attack and stroke
Adrenal suppression

=== SLIDE 11 ===
Clinical Considerations for Glucocorticoids
NSAIDs interaction – Increased risk of GI bleed

Loop and Thiazide Diuretics interaction – Increased risk of hypokalemia

Warfarin (Coumadin) – May enhance or reduce effect of anticoagulation

Diabetics – Increased blood sugar

Older patients – Increased risk of fractures

Increased risk of infections

Long term use requires tapering of dose to prevent adrenal insufficiency
**Medication needs to be tapered and weaned off to allow for the adrenal gland to make its own cortisol.**

=== SLIDE 13 ===
Pharmacological Action
Suppressing the immune system's overactivity and inflammatory response, which is a key factor in many autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis. 
DMARDs are often used in conjunction with glucocorticoids and nsaids to provide symptomatic relief and slow progression of disease.

Therapeutic Uses
Analgesia for pain, swelling, and joint stiffness 
Maintenance of joint function
Slow/delay the worsening of the disease 
Prevention of organ rejection in clients who have transplants (kidney, liver, and heart transplants 
Management of inflammatory bowel disease
