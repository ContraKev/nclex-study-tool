Normal head circumference
32-36 cm (12.5 – 14.1 inches)
Should not be more than 2 cm larger than the chest
Fontanelles
should be flat with the contour of the skull
Bulging indicates increased intracranial pressure
Depressed indicates dehydration
Birth injuries
Molding – overlapping skull bones from birth canal pressure
Caput succedaneum- swelling of soft tissues of scalp
Cephalohematoma- bleeding beneath the periosteum of the cranial bone
Does not cross suture lines
Should recede within a few weeks

=== SLIDE 12 ===
Apgar Score (2 of 2)
Score is obtained at 1 and 5 minutes after birth.
Nurse monitors for respiratory distress as evidenced by
Rate and character of respirations
Color (cyanosis)
General behavior
Sternal retractions must be reported immediately to the health care provider.

=== SLIDE 16 ===
Providing Warmth
Unstable heat-regulating system
Normal Temperature: 36.2-37.7 C (97.1-99.8 F)
Acrocyanosis is evident because of sluggish peripheral circulation.
Cannot adapt to change in temperatures easily
Sweat glands do not function during neonatal period, so infant is at risk for developing elevated temperature if overdressed or placed in overheated environment.

=== SLIDE 25 ===
Preventing Infections
Newborn’s response to inflammation and infection is slow because of the immaturity of the immune system.
Umbilical cord stump is a primary site of infection if not kept clean.
Hand hygiene is the primary means of preventing infection and its spread.
