OB REVIEW - Instructor Notes (Rebecca Villagrana)
CMS on all OB, Exam on Ch 3,4,5

Key Topics Highlighted:
- Bright red painless bleeding = placenta previa
- Painful bleeding = placenta abruption
- Ectopic pregnancy: pelvic pain, uterus not large (growing in tube)
- Heavy red vaginal bleeding + feel fetus move = suspecting placenta abruption, dx: ultrasound
- RhoGAM: medication given when mom Rh-negative and fetus Rh-positive
- No rubella vaccine during pregnancy (live vaccine) - give after delivery
- How to take ferrous sulfate: with OJ (vitamin C enhances absorption)
- Quickening: first movement of fetus at 4-5 months
- Missed miscarriage: fetus dies in utero, requires abortion/delivery
- Placenta previa: do NOT do vaginal exam (risk of hemorrhage)
- Preeclampsia: proteinuria, hypertension, headache, blurry vision
- First trimester: urinary urgency and frequency
- Folic acid: 400 mcg daily
- Terbutaline: know side effects
- Signs of impending labor: increase in vaginal fluids, surge of energy/nesting, lose weight, urinary frequency
- Reduce stress level good for mom, monitor weight gain, some meds OK
- Amniocentesis: US guided to avoid fetus and placenta
- Smoking can lead to preterm birth and low birth weight
- Abdominal pain + vaginal bleeding = placenta abruption
- Maternal diabetes: baby at risk for hypoglycemia
- GTPAL: parity = 20+ weeks delivery
- Alpha fetoprotein (AFP) screening detects neural tube defects
- Right after delivery: fundal height at umbilicus; at 20 weeks fundal height at umbilicus
- Lordosis (curve in spine) causes low back pain in pregnancy
- Leukorrhea: vaginal discharge normal while pregnant
- Constipation normal in pregnancy
- Presumptive, probable, and positive signs of pregnancy
- Nagele's rule: minus 3 months plus 7 days
- Most common risk factor for placenta abruption: HYPERTENSION
- Magnesium sulfate: monitor RESPIRATORY RATE
- GTPAL calculations
