=== SLIDE 1 ===
Chapter 13
Preterm and Postterm Newborns

=== SLIDE 2 ===
Lesson 13.1 Objectives
Define each key word listed.
Differentiate between the preterm and the low-birthweight newborn.
List three causes of preterm birth.
Describe selected problems of preterm birth and the nursing goals associated with each problem.

=== KEY TERMS ===
IUGR (Intrauterine Growth Restriction)
SGA (Small for Gestational Age) - weight below 10th percentile
LGA (Large for Gestational Age) - weight above 90th percentile or >4000g
Ballard Score - estimates gestational age based on external characteristics and neurological development
Surfactant - fatty protein necessary for lung oxygen absorption
RDS (Respiratory Distress Syndrome) - hyaline membrane disease from lung immaturity
Apnea - cessation of breathing >20 seconds
Sepsis - generalized bloodstream infection
NEC (Necrotizing Enterocolitis) - acute inflammation and necrosis of bowel
Kernicterus - bilirubin encephalopathy, neurological damage
Kangaroo Care - skin-to-skin contact

=== GESTATIONAL AGE CLASSIFICATIONS ===
Preterm: <37 weeks
Early term: 37 weeks 0 days - 38 weeks 6 days
Full term: 39 weeks 0 days - 40 weeks 6 days
Late term: 41 weeks 0 days - 41 weeks 6 days
Postterm: ≥42 weeks

=== CAUSES OF PRETERM BIRTH ===
Multiple births
Maternal illness (GH, infections)
Placental abnormalities (previa, abruption)
Premature rupture of membranes
Infection/inflammation
Uterine anomalies
Substance abuse
Chronic stress, inadequate prenatal care

=== PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS OF PRETERM INFANTS ===
Transparent, loose skin
Superficial veins visible
Lack of subcutaneous fat
Lanugo covers forehead, shoulders, arms
Abundant vernix
Short extremities
Few sole creases
Genitalia may be immature

=== MAJOR PROBLEMS OF PRETERM INFANTS ===

1. RESPIRATORY DISTRESS SYNDROME (RDS)
- Caused by surfactant deficiency
- Signs: RR >60, grunting, nasal flaring, cyanosis, retractions
- Treatment: Surfactant via ET tube, O2 therapy, mechanical ventilation
- Betamethasone/dexamethasone before delivery (24-48h) increases surfactant production

2. APNEA
- Breathing cessation >20 seconds
- Often with bradycardia and cyanosis
- Treatment: Gentle stimulation, suction, semi-Fowler's position, Ambu bag if needed

3. SEPSIS
- Preterm infants at high risk (immature immune system, poor antibody formation)
- Signs: Low temp, lethargy/irritability, poor feeding, respiratory distress
- Treatment: IV antimicrobials, maintain warmth, cluster care, hand hygiene

4. THERMOREGULATION PROBLEMS
- Lack of brown fat and subcutaneous insulation
- High metabolism, cannot shiver
- Skin temp falls before core temp
- Treatment: Incubator/radiant warmer, skin probe, optimal temp 36.2-37°C

5. HYPOGLYCEMIA
- <40 mg/dL (term) or <30 mg/dL (preterm)
- Preterm infants have low glycogen/fat stores
- Treatment: Frequent glucose monitoring, NG or IV feedings

6. HYPOCALCEMIA
- Early: parathyroid fails to respond to low calcium
- Late: cow's milk increases phosphate, drops calcium
- Treatment: IV calcium gluconate (monitor for bradycardia)

7. RETINOPATHY OF PREMATURITY (ROP)
- Disorder of developing retina → blindness
- Risk: <1500g, oxygen exposure
- Prevention: Monitor O2 sat, maintain safe levels, vitamin E

8. NECROTIZING ENTEROCOLITIS (NEC)
- Acute bowel inflammation → necrosis
- Signs: Abdominal distention, bloody stools, bilious vomiting, diarrhea
- Treatment: NPO, IV fluids, antibiotics, possible surgery

9. JAUNDICE
- Physiological vs pathological
- Preterm: slower bilirubin rise, lasts longer → hyperbilirubinemia risk
- Treatment: Phototherapy, increase fluids, prevent kernicterus

=== NURSING CARE FOR PRETERM INFANTS ===
BUBBLE-type assessment adapted for newborns
Cluster care to minimize handling
Kangaroo care (skin-to-skin) - promotes bonding, thermoregulation
Position: Side or prone, HOB elevated slightly
Frequent position changes to prevent skin breakdown
NG/gavage feeding for very premature (<1500g)
Observe bowel sounds, meconium passage
Monitor glucose, bilirubin, electrolytes
Support parents - teach care, address guilt, encourage bonding

=== FEEDING THE PRETERM INFANT ===
<34 weeks: May lack coordination of sucking, breathing, swallowing
Breast milk is ideal (fat absorbed easily)
Gavage feeding: tube through nose/mouth to stomach for very premature
>1500g (3.3lb): May bottle feed with small soft nipple, large hole
IV fluids or TPN if too premature/ill for oral feeds

=== KANGAROO CARE ===
Skin-to-skin contact with parent
Infant placed on parent's bare chest
Promotes bonding, thermoregulation, stability
Enhances development and breastfeeding success

=== POSTTERM NEWBORN (≥42 weeks) ===
Placenta functions poorly → decreased blood flow, possible distress
Higher mortality and morbidity than term infants

PROBLEMS:
- Asphyxia
- Meconium aspiration
- Poor nutritional status (wasted appearance)
- Polycythemia (↑ RBC production)
- Difficult delivery (larger size)
- Birth defects, seizures

PHYSICAL CHARACTERISTICS:
- Long and thin
- Loose skin (especially buttocks/thighs)
- Little lanugo and vernix
- Dry, cracked, peeling skin
- Long nails (may be meconium-stained)
- Thick head of hair, alert appearance

=== TRANSPORT OF HIGH-RISK NEWBORN ===
Stabilize before transport
Obtain baseline VS and labs
Copy all records (maternal prenatal, delivery)
Ensure proper identification (matching bands)
Provide parents: NICU name, location, phone numbers
Allow brief visit with infant before transport
Take photo for parents

=== DISCHARGE PLANNING ===
Begins at birth
Parents demonstrate routine and specialized care
Home nursing visits may be needed
Discuss behavioral patterns, realistic expectations
Review newborn CPR
Support group referrals
Warm lines for phone support
Social services assessment of home environment

=== REVIEW KEY POINTS ===
Preterm = <37 weeks; Postterm = ≥42 weeks
RDS caused by surfactant deficiency → treat with surfactant + O2
Apnea = no breathing >20 sec → stimulate, suction, position
Preterm infants prone to: sepsis, hypothermia, hypoglycemia, hypocalcemia
NEC: bloody stools, abdominal distention, bilious vomiting
ROP: monitor O2 sat carefully, vitamin E
Kangaroo care promotes bonding and thermoregulation
Postterm: dry peeling skin, little vernix, meconium staining
