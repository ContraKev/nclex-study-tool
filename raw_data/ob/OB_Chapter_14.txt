=== SLIDE 1 ===
Chapter 14
The Newborn with a Perinatal Injury or Congenital Malformation

=== KEY TERMS ===
Birth defects, Congenital malformations, Hydrocephalus, Spina bifida, Meningocele, Meningomyelocele
Cleft lip, Cleft palate, Cheiloplasty, Clubfoot (Talipes equinovarus)
Developmental hip dysplasia, Pavlik harness, Ortolani sign, Barlow test
PKU (Phenylketonuria), Galactosemia, Maple syrup urine disease
Erythroblastosis fetalis, RhoGAM, Phototherapy, Kernicterus
Macrosomia, Meconium aspiration syndrome (MAS), Neonatal abstinence syndrome (NAS)
Transient tachypnea of newborn (TTN), Down syndrome

=== CLASSIFICATIONS OF BIRTH DEFECTS ===
1. Malformations present at birth (structural, functional, metabolic)
2. Inborn errors of metabolism
3. Disorders of the blood
4. Chromosomal abnormalities
5. Perinatal injuries

=== HYDROCEPHALUS ===
Definition: Increase in CSF within brain ventricles → ↑ intracranial pressure, ↑ head size
Causes: Imbalance in CSF production/absorption, improper ventricle formation
Classifications: Noncommunicating (obstruction) vs Communicating (poor reabsorption)

SIGNS OF INCREASING ICP IN INFANTS:
- ↑ BP, ↓ pulse, ↓ respirations
- High-pitched cry
- Unequal pupils
- Bulging fontanelles
- Irritability or lethargy
- Vomiting, poor feeding

MANIFESTATIONS:
- Enlargement of head
- Cranial sutures separate
- Scalp shiny, veins dilated

TREATMENT:
- Medications to reduce CSF production
- VP shunt (ventriculoperitoneal) - bypass obstruction
- Shunt may need revision if infection or malfunction

NURSING CARE:
Preoperative: Frequent head position changes, support head always, measure head circumference
Postoperative: Assess for ↑ ICP, protect from infection, depress shunt pump as ordered, position appropriately, pain control
Parent education: Signs of shunt malfunction, how to pump shunt

=== SPINA BIFIDA (MYELODYSPLASIA) ===
Definition: Malformation of spinal cord, imperfect closure of spinal vertebrae

TYPES:
- Occulta (hidden): Mild, often asymptomatic
- Cystica: Sac or cyst present
  - Meningocele: Contains membranes and CSF
  - Meningomyelocele: Contains membranes AND spinal cord → paralysis risk

PREVENTION: Folic acid 0.4 mg/day before pregnancy and through 12th week

TREATMENT: Surgical closure; prognosis depends on spinal cord involvement

=== CLEFT LIP AND PALATE ===
CLEFT LIP: Fissure in upper lip; failure of maxillary and median nasal processes to unite
- More common in boys
- Often hereditary

CLEFT PALATE: Failure of hard palates to fuse at midline (7th-12th week)
- Passageway between nasopharynx and nose
- ↑ Risk of respiratory infections, otitis media

TREATMENT:
- Cheiloplasty (lip repair): By 3 months of age
- Elbow restraints to prevent scratching
- Special feeding devices until surgery

POSTOPERATIVE CARE:
- Progressive diet advancement
- No straws (prevents sucking)
- Oral hygiene: Water after each feeding
- Speech therapy

=== CLUBFOOT (TALIPES EQUINOVARUS) ===
Most common congenital foot deformity
Foot twists inward or outward; generally bilateral

TREATMENT:
- Conservative: Splinting, casting, passive stretching exercises
- If not effective after ~3 months → surgery

NURSING: Stress compliance with cast care, skin breakdown prevention

=== DEVELOPMENTAL HIP DYSPLASIA ===
Definition: Head of femur partly or completely displaced from acetabulum
7x more common in girls; apparent when infant begins walking

DIAGNOSTICS:
- Barlow test: Adduction/extension → feel dislocation occur
- Ortolani sign: Feel/hear femoral head slip back into acetabulum

TREATMENT:
- Pavlik harness: Maintains flexion and abduction for 4-8 weeks
- Keeps femoral head in socket → deepens acetabulum
- Surgery if conservative fails

=== METABOLIC DEFECTS (INBORN ERRORS OF METABOLISM) ===
Genetic defects not apparent until after birth

SIGNS TO REPORT:
- Lethargy, poor feeding, hypotonia
- Unique odor to body/urine
- Tachypnea, vomiting

PHENYLKETONURIA (PKU):
- Faulty metabolism of phenylalanine (amino acid in all protein)
- Phenylalanine accumulates → severe mental retardation if untreated
- Newborn screening test mandatory
- Treatment: Special diet low in phenylalanine; avoid aspartame

GALACTOSEMIA:
- Cannot metabolize galactose/lactose
- Enzyme missing to convert galactose → glucose
- Untreated: Cirrhosis, cataracts, mental retardation
- Treatment: Eliminate milk/lactose; use soy-based formula

MAPLE SYRUP URINE DISEASE:
- Cannot metabolize branched-chain amino acids
- Urine has maple syrup odor
- Treatment: Special diet

=== DOWN SYNDROME ===
Most common chromosomal abnormality
Risk ↑ with maternal age ≥35, paternal age ≥55
Mild to severe mental retardation

PHYSICAL FEATURES:
- Flat facial profile, epicanthal folds
- Single palmar crease
- Hypotonia, short stature
- Protruding tongue

DEVELOPMENT: Milestones delayed (sitting, walking, talking slower than normal)

=== ERYTHROBLASTOSIS FETALIS (HEMOLYTIC DISEASE) ===
Cause: Rh-negative mother, Rh-positive fetus → maternal antibodies destroy fetal RBCs

MANIFESTATIONS:
- Anemia from hemolysis
- Pathological jaundice within 24 hours (bilirubin rises rapidly)
- Hepatosplenomegaly
- Risk of shock or heart failure

DIAGNOSIS: Direct Coombs test on umbilical cord blood

TREATMENT:
- Phototherapy for jaundice
- Exchange transfusion if severe
- RhoGAM given to Rh-negative mothers at 28 weeks and within 72h after delivery

=== PHOTOTHERAPY ===
Used to reduce serum bilirubin levels
Breaks down bilirubin → excreted

NURSING CARE:
- Cover eyes with protective patches (prevent retinal damage)
- Maximum skin exposure (diaper only)
- Monitor temperature (risk of hyperthermia)
- Increase fluid intake
- Turn infant frequently
- Home phototherapy may be used for mild cases

=== INTRACRANIAL HEMORRHAGE ===
Most common type of birth injury
Causes: Trauma, anoxia, precipitate delivery, prolonged labor
More common in preterm infants

SIGNS: Vary with severity; may include seizures, apnea, bulging fontanelle, altered consciousness

=== TRANSIENT TACHYPNEA OF NEWBORN (TTN) ===
Self-limiting; resolves suddenly within ~3 days
Caused by delayed clearance of fetal lung fluid
Treatment: Supportive (O2, observation)

=== MECONIUM ASPIRATION SYNDROME (MAS) ===
In utero: Fetus expels meconium into amniotic fluid
Asphyxia → gasping movements → meconium drawn into lungs

At delivery: If newborn inhales before suctioning → aspiration

SIGNS: Respiratory distress, meconium-stained amniotic fluid
TREATMENT: Immediate suctioning, possibly mechanical ventilation

=== NEONATAL ABSTINENCE SYNDROME (NAS) ===
Cause: Fetal exposure to drugs in utero (opioids, etc.)
Illicit drugs cross placenta → infant experiences withdrawal after birth

SIGNS: High-pitched cry, tremors, sneezing, vomiting, diarrhea, seizures

TREATMENT: Supportive care, medications to manage withdrawal, swaddling, low-stimulation environment

LONG-TERM: Risk of developmental and neurological deficits

=== INFANT OF DIABETIC MOTHER (IDM) / MACROSOMIA ===
Large amounts of glucose cross placenta → fetal hyperglycemia
Fetal pancreas produces excess insulin → hyperinsulinism

RESULTS: Macrosomia (birth weight >4000g or 9 lb)
After birth: Newborn becomes hypoglycemic (BS <45 mg/dL) as insulin continues

NURSING: Frequent glucose monitoring, early feeding

=== REVIEW KEY POINTS ===
Hydrocephalus: VP shunt; monitor for ↑ ICP signs
Spina bifida: Folic acid prevention; meningocele vs myelomeningocele
Cleft lip: Cheiloplasty by 3 months; special feeding
Cleft palate: ↑ Otitis media risk; speech therapy
Clubfoot: Casting/splinting; compliance essential
Hip dysplasia: Pavlik harness; Barlow and Ortolani tests
PKU: Newborn screening; low phenylalanine diet
Down syndrome: ↑ Maternal age risk; delayed milestones
Erythroblastosis: RhoGAM prevention; phototherapy treatment
MAS: Suction immediately at delivery
NAS: Drug withdrawal; supportive care
IDM: Macrosomia → hypoglycemia after birth
