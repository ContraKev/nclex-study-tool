Determining the Estimated Date of Delivery
Average pregnancy is 40 weeks (280 days) after first day of LMP, plus or minus 2 weeks
Nägele’s rule
Identify first day of LMP. (last normal menstrual period aka first day of bleeding) 
Count backward 3 months.
Add 7 days.
Update year, if applicable.

=== SLIDE 38 ===
Changes of pregnancy
Vagina: blood supply increases, mucosa thickens, rugae become more prominent. 
Vaginal secretions increase. pH becomes more acidic.
Breasts: hormone induced breast changes occur early in pregnancy. High levels of estrogen and progesterone prepare the breasts for lactation. The areolae of the breasts become deeply pigmented, sebaceous glands in nipples become prominent, colostrum may be expressed in the last few months of pregnancy

Four nutrients especially important in pregnancy: protein, calcium, iron and folic acid

=== SLIDE 49 ===
Nutrition Requirements for Pregnant Women

=== SLIDE 50 ===
Daily Food Pattern for Pregnancy
Protein: Added protein is needed: an increase to 60 grams/day is recommended during pregnancy
Calcium: DRI is 1000mg. An adequate intake of Vitamin D is required to enhance calcium absorption. (DRI = Daily Recommended Intake)
Iron: 30mg/day for pregnant women (15 mg/day nonpregnant)
Folic Acid: is a water-soluble B vitamin (folacin or folate) reduces the incidence of Neural tube defects, DRI 400mcg/day (0-4 months), then 600-800 mcg/day (4-9 months)

=== SLIDE 56 ===
Nutritional Requirements During Lactation

=== SLIDE 57 ===
Protein rich foods
Added protein is needed for metabolism and to support growth and repair of maternal and fetal tissues. 
The best sources are meat, fish, poultry, and dairy products.

=== SLIDE 59 ===
Calcium Rich Foods
Dairy products are the most plentiful source of this nutrient.
Other sources of calcium include enriched cereals, legumes, nuts, dried fruits, broccoli, green leafy vegetables, canned salmon and sardines

=== SLIDE 60 ===
Vitamins and Minerals
True vitamin deficiency is rare in North America.
Excess Vitamin A is teratogenic…and can cause fetal anomalies and cardiac defects.
Copper, zinc, folates, vitamin C, Vitamin D all need to be considered. (and hopefully are included in the patient's prenatal vitamin)
Vitamin B6 (pyroxidine) and ginger are often recommended to relieve the common discomfort of nausea in pregnancy

Pregnancy travel safety
Seat belt? Yes! Lap belt goes under the belly, fitting snugly across the hips/pelvic bone. Shoulder straps shouldn’t go across directly maternal abdomen, but between breasts and off to the side of the belly.
Frequent breaks (stretch breaks and pee breaks)
Hydration
Avoid staying in same position (increased risk for DVTs)
Range of motion exercises for feet/legs/compression socks can help as well

In Summary
Prenatal care is how we improve outcomes for women and their families
It incorporates many aspects of a women’s life
Physical, social, genetic factors are all critical to evaluate
Pre-conceptual planning is helpful for some women
Risks for medications and immunizations in pregnancy can be lessened by awareness and communication with provider. 
Nurses are an integral part of educating family unit.
