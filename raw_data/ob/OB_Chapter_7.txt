=== SLIDE 18 ===
BRAXTON HICKS
Braxton Hicks contractions are false labor contractions you can have during pregnancy. Unlike true labor contractions, Braxton Hicks are irregular in frequency, less intense and usually go away if you change positions.

Fetal Fibronectin (fFN) Test 
Purpose: 
The fFN test is a screening test used to assess the risk of preterm labor in pregnant women. It detects a protein called fetal fibronectin (fFN) in vaginal fluid or cervical mucus.

Nursing Care Before Birth
After admission to the labor unit, nursing care consists of
Monitoring the fetus
Monitoring the laboring woman

=== SLIDE 29 ===
Monitoring the Fetus
Fetal heart rate (FHR)
Monitor every 30 minutes in active, first phase of labor.
Monitor every 15 minutes in second stage and preferably before each contraction.
Risk factors present
Monitor every 15 minutes in active first stage.
Monitor every 5 minutes during second stage.
Intermittent auscultation
Continuous electronic fetal monitoring

=== SLIDE 30 ===
Fetal Heart Rate Outside of Normal Limits
Any FHR outside the normal limits and any slowing of the FHR that persists after the contraction ends is promptly reported to the health care provider.

=== SLIDE 38 ===
Madelyn, please read this
Lamaze is a childbirth education method that focuses on giving parents confidence through techniques like controlled breathing, movement, and relaxation to help manage labor and delivery. While it is well-known for breathing patterns, modern Lamaze classes provide a comprehensive approach covering physical and emotional preparation, comfort measures, and evidence-based practices for a safer, healthier birth with less unnecessary medical intervention.

=== SLIDE 39 ===
Six Lamaze Institute Basic Practices for Maternity Care
Labor should begin on its own.
No routine interventions should be performed.
Woman should:
Have freedom of movement.
Have a birth support person or doula.
Be in nonsupine positions.
Not be separated from infant.

Stages and Phases of Labor
First stage—dilation and effacement (can last 4 to 6 hours)

Fourth stage—recovery

=== SLIDE 60 ===
Umbilical Cord Blood Banking
This type of blood is capable of regenerating stem cells that are able to replace diseased cells.
Informed consent is essential.
Collect blood after cord has been clamped.
Blood must be transported within 48 hours of collection to blood banking facility.
