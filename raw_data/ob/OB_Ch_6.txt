=== SLIDE 16 ===
More Signs of Impending Labor
Lightening/Dropping: The baby drops lower into the pelvis, which can relieve heartburn but increase pressure on the bladder.
Loose Bowel Movements: Muscles in the rectum may relax, leading to diarrhea or frequent loose stools.
Increased Energy/Nesting: A sudden, brief burst of energy.
Loss of Mucus Plug: Often occurs days or hours before labor begins.

=== SLIDE 19 ===
The 3-2-1 contraction rule is a guideline for first-time mothers to know when to head to the hospital: contractions are consistently 3 minutes apart, lasting 2 minutes (or 60 seconds) each, for at least 1 hour. It's a more conservative version of the common 5-1-1 rule (5 mins apart, 1 min long, 1 hr) used for subsequent pregnancies or by providers aiming for less intervention, helping distinguish true labor from false labor.

Monitor every 30 minutes in active, first phase of labor.

Risk factors present

Intermittent auscultation
Continuous electronic fetal monitoring

=== SLIDE 28 ===
Fetal Heart Rate Outside of Normal Limits
Any FHR outside the normal limits and any slowing of the FHR that persists after the contraction ends is promptly reported to the health care provider.

This type of blood is capable of regenerating stem cells that are able to replace diseased cells.
Informed consent is essential.

Blood must be transported within 48 hours of collection to blood banking facility.
