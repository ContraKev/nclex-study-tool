## 🤱 CHAPTER 9: THE FAMILY AFTER BIRTH

### BUBBLE Postpartum Assessment ⭐
| Letter | Assessment | Key Points |
|--------|------------|------------|
| **B** | Breasts | Soft/engorged; nipples intact |
| **U** | Uterus | Firm, fundal height (↓1-2cm/day) |
| **B** | Bladder | No distention |
| **B** | Bowels | Gas, bowel sounds, stool |
| **L** | Lochia | Rubra → Serosa → Alba |
| **E** | Episiotomy/Perineum | REEDA |
| **+E** | Emotions | Bonding, support |

### Lochia Timeline
| Type | Color | Duration |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Rubra** | 🔴 Red | Days 1-3 |
| **Serosa** | 🟣 Pink | Days 4-10 |
| **Alba** | ⚪ White | Days 10-21 |

> Assess pads AND under patient — blood can overflow!

### Uterine Involution
- **1-2 cm/day** descent
- **10 days**: No longer palpable
- **5-6 weeks**: Back to pre-pregnant size
- **Massage** if boggy!

### Perineum Comfort
| Time | Intervention |
|------|--------------|
| **First 12-24 hrs** | 🧊 Ice pack |
| **After 24 hrs** | 🛁 Sitz bath |

### Breastfeeding Hormones
| Hormone | Source | Function |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **Prolactin** | Anterior pituitary | Milk **production** |
| **Oxytocin** | Posterior pituitary | Let-down reflex (milk ejection) |

**Contraindications**: HIV, HTLV-1 & 2

### Milk Stages
| Stage | Timing | Color/Notes |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Colostrum** | Days 1-3 | 🟡 Yellow, thick, immunoglobulins |
| **Transitional** | Days 7-10 | ↑ Lactose |
| **Mature** | Day 14+ | 🔵 Bluish, 20 kcal/oz |

**Foremilk** = watery, thirst-quenching
**Hindmilk** = fatty, satisfying

### Newborn Heat Loss (4 Types)
| Type | Cause | Prevention |
|------|-------|------------|
| **Evaporation** | Wet skin | Dry immediately |
| **Conduction** | Cold surface | Warm blankets |
| **Convection** | Drafts | Close doors, no fans |
| **Radiation** | Cool objects | Radiant warmer, hat |

### Newborn Hypoglycemia
**<45 mg/dL** in term infant

**Risks**: Preterm/postterm, diabetic mom, LGA/SGA, IUGR, cold stress

**S/S**: Jittery, poor tone, sweating, high-pitched cry, seizures

### Cord Care
- Delayed clamping 30-60 sec
- Keep **clean & dry**
- Diaper below cord
- Sponge bath until falls off

## 🚨 CHAPTER 10: POSTPARTUM COMPLICATIONS

### Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH) ⭐
| Birth Type | PPH Threshold |
|------------|---------------|
| Vaginal | >500 mL |
| C-Section | >1000 mL |

**Timing**: Early (<24h) vs Late (24h-6wk)

### 4 T's of PPH ⭐
| Cause | Most Common | Treatment |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Tone** | Uterine atony | Massage, oxytocin, Methergine |
| **Trauma** | Lacerations | Surgical repair |
| **Tissue** | Retained placenta | D&C |
| **Thrombin** | Coagulopathy | Blood products, correct cause |

### Atony vs. Laceration
| Finding | Atony | Laceration |
|---------|-------|------------|
| Uterus | **Boggy** | Firm |
| Bleeding | Dark red, clots | **Bright red**, trickle |
| Fundus | High | Normal |

### Hypovolemic Shock Signs
> ⚠️ **Tachycardia FIRST** (before BP drops!)

| Sign | Finding |
|------|---------|
| Pulse | ⬆️ Tachycardia |
| BP | ⬇️ Hypotension |
| Skin | Pale, cold, clammy |
| Mental status | Altered |
| Urine output | ⬇️ Decreased |

### Thromboembolic Disorders
| Type | Location | Key S/S |
|------|----------|---------|
| **SVT** | Superficial (saphenous) | Localized redness, warmth |
| **DVT** | Deep (femoral) | Leg circumference >2cm diff, edema, pain |
| **PE** | Pulmonary artery | Sudden chest pain, SOB, hemoptysis |

**Prevention**: Early ambulation, SCDs, no popliteal pressure

**Anticoagulant Teaching**:
- Soft toothbrush
- Report bleeding/bruising
- Vitamin K = warfarin antidote
- No LMWH with epidural

### Puerperal Infection
**Definition**: Fever ≥100.4°F (38°C) in first 24h AND ≥2 days in first 10 days

**Most common**: **Endometritis**

**Prevention**: Hand hygiene, front→back peripad

### Mastitis
**S/S**: Unilateral breast redness, warmth, pain + flu-like symptoms

**Treatment** (5 cornerstones):
1. ✅ Antibiotics
2. ✅ **Continue breastfeeding/pumping**
3. ✅ Analgesics
4. ✅ Heat before feeding
5. ✅ Cold after feeding

> ⚠️ **Do NOT stop nursing** — worsens engorgement!

### Postpartum Mood Disorders
| Disorder | Onset | Duration | Severity |
|----------|-------|----------|----------|
| **Blues** | Days 2-3 | Resolves by day 10 | Mild, normal |
| **Depression** | 2-4 weeks | Weeks-months | Moderate-severe |
| **Psychosis** | <2 weeks | **Emergency!** | Severe — delusions, hallucinations |

**Never assume "just baby blues"** — assess for persistence

## 👶 CHAPTER 12: THE TERM NEWBORN

### Apgar Score ⭐
Scored at **1 and 5 minutes**

| Sign | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|------|---|---|---|
| HR | Absent | <100 | >100 |
| Resp | Absent | Slow | Good, cry |
| Tone | Limp | Some flex | Active |
| Reflex | None | Grimace | Cry |
| Color | Blue/pale | Acrocyanosis | Pink |

**7-10** = Normal | **4-6** = Moderate | **0-3** = Severe distress

### Newborn Vital Signs
| Parameter | Normal |
|-----------|--------|
| **Temp** | 97.1-99.8°F (36.2-37.7°C) |
| **HR** | 110-160 bpm |
| **RR** | 30-60/min |
| **BP** | 60-80/40-50 |

> Acrocyanosis (blue hands/feet) = normal!

### Fontanelles
| Type | Shape | Closes |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Anterior** | Diamond | ~18 months |
| **Posterior** | Triangle | ~2-3 months |

- **Bulging** = ↑ ICP
- **Depressed** = Dehydration

### Birth Injuries
| Condition | Crosses Sutures? | Resolves |
|-----------|------------------|----------|
| **Molding** | N/A | Hours-days |
| **Caput** | ✅ Yes | Days |
| **Cephalohematoma** | ❌ No | Weeks |

### Newborn Reflexes
| Reflex | Disappears |
|--------|------------|
| **Rooting** | 3-4 mo |
| **Sucking** | 4 mo |
| **Moro** | 4-6 mo |
| **Grasp** | 3-4 mo |
| **Babinski** | 12 mo |
| **Stepping** | 2 mo |
| **Tonic Neck** | 4-6 mo |

### Pain Scales
- **FLACC** (Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability) — commonly used
- **CRIES**, **NIPS**, **NPASS**

### Newborn Nutrition
| Stool | Appearance |
|-------|------------|
| **Meconium** | Black, tarry (first 24-48h) |
| **Transitional** | Greenish-brown |
| **Breastfed** | 🟡 Yellow, seedy, soft |
| **Formula** | 🟤 Pale yellow/tan, firmer |

**Vitamin D** commonly prescribed for breastfed infants

### Danger Signs — Mother
- Saturating >1 pad/hr
- Foul-smelling lochia
- Temp >100.4°F
- Severe headache, vision changes
- Chest pain, SOB
- Leg swelling/redness
- Thoughts of harming self/baby

### Danger Signs — Newborn
- Temp <97°F or >100.4°F
- Grunting, retractions, cyanosis
- Seizures, jitteriness
- Poor feeding, lethargy
- No wet diaper 12h / no stool 48h
- Jaundice in first 24h
- Cord redness, foul odor
