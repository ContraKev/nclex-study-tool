# ATI CMS Peds Practice A Cheatsheet

**Source:** Quizlet/Studocu "ATI CMS Pediatric Practice A"

## BURNS
- Phases: Emergent (shock, airway, fluids 24-48hr), Intermediate (infection, wound closure, pain), Rehabilitative (contractures, mobility, psych)
- Priority: ABCs, humidified 100% O2, fluid resuscitation (LR), urine output 1-2mL/kg/hr
- Pain: IV opioids; no IM
- Infection: reverse isolation, no plants/flowers
- Nutrition: high-calorie, high-protein
- Prevent contractures: ROM, splinting, pressure garments

## RESPIRATORY
- Croup: barky cough, stridor; cool mist, racemic epi, steroids; keep calm
- Epiglottitis: drooling, tripod, high fever; EMERGENCY — do NOT examine throat
- Bronchiolitis (RSV): infants; wheezing, apnea; suction, hydration, O2
- Asthma: albuterol rescue, peak flow, triggers (allergens, cold, exercise); status asthmaticus = emergency
- Early distress: restlessness, tachypnea, nasal flaring, retractions, grunting
- Late distress: bradycardia, cyanosis, stupor

## GI/NUTRITION
- Celiac: gluten-free lifelong; failure to thrive, diarrhea, distention
- Lactose intolerance: cramps, flatulence, diarrhea after dairy; yogurt/cheese often tolerated
- Enteral feeding: verify placement (pH, X-ray); HOB ≥30°; check residuals; flush before/after
- Pyloric stenosis: projectile vomiting, olive mass
- Intussusception: currant jelly stools, sausage mass
- Hirschsprung: ribbon stools, failure to pass meconium

## MUSCULOSKELETAL
- Scoliosis: Adams test (rib hump); bracing 20-40°; surgery >40°
- DDH: Ortolani/Barlow, asymmetric folds; Pavlik harness
- JIA: morning stiffness, swelling, limping; NSAIDs, DMARDs, PT; eye exams (uveitis)
- Fractures: 5 P's neuro checks; cast care

## MEDICATIONS
- Topical/transdermal: clean/dry skin, rotate sites
- Eye drops: occlude nasolacrimal duct 1min; wait 5min between meds
- Ear drops: pull pinna (DOWN/BACK infant, UP/BACK child)
- Transfusion reaction: STOP immediately; fever, chills, back pain, tachycardia
- Digoxin: hold if HR<90 infant, <70 child; toxicity: vomiting, bradycardia, visual changes

## INFECTIOUS
- Meningitis: fever, nuchal rigidity, Kernig/Brudzinski; CSF cloudy (bacterial); droplet 24hr; antibiotics after cultures
- Reye: aspirin + viral → encephalopathy; avoid aspirin in children
- Varicella: vesicular rash; contagious until crusted
- Fifth disease: slapped cheek → lacy rash; dangerous in pregnancy
- Roseola: high fever → rash after fever drops
- Scarlet: sandpaper rash, strawberry tongue
- Impetigo: contagious; hand hygiene
- Mononucleosis: fever, lymphadenopathy, splenomegaly; avoid contact sports

## SPECIFIC CONDITIONS
- Kawasaki: fever>5d, strawberry tongue, rash, conjunctivitis; IVIG + aspirin; coronary aneurysm risk
- Leukemia: fever, bruising, bone pain; ANC<500 = neutropenia; neutropenic fever = emergency
- Sickle cell: hydration, pain mgmt; crisis = IV fluids, O2, analgesics; avoid ice/cold
- Hemophilia: factor VIII, ice/elevation; avoid NSAIDs/aspirin
- Diabetes: polyuria/polydipsia/polyphagia; sick days: check glucose, NEVER skip insulin; DKA = fruity breath
- Hydroceph: bulging fontanel, increased HC, sunset eyes; VP shunt malfunction: vomiting, headache, altered LOC
- Seizures: side-lying, protect head, time, NO restraint; rescue med if ordered

## PAIN ASSESSMENT
- FLACC: infants/nonverbal
- FACES: 3+ years
- Numeric: 7+ years
- Premedicate 30-60min before painful procedures

## GROWTH & DEVELOPMENT
- Milestones: HC most important infant; weight doubles 6mo, triples 12mo
- Erikson: Trust (infant), Autonomy (toddler), Initiative (preschool), Industry (school-age), Identity (adolescent)
- Piaget: Sensorimotor, Preoperational, Concrete Operational, Formal Operational
- Play: parallel (toddler), cooperative (preschool), competitive (school-age)

## HOSPITALIZED CHILD
- Regression: normal, temporary
- Separation anxiety: Protest→Despair→Detachment
- Preparation by age: age-appropriate explanations
- Atraumatic care: minimize separation/pain, promote control
- FCC: family is constant/expert

## SAFETY
- SIDS: back to sleep, firm mattress, no co-sleeping
- Car seat: rear-facing until age 2 AND 20-22lb
- Burns: water heater ≤120°F
- Poison: lock cabinets
- Drowning: supervise, empty pools/bathtubs
- Abuse: spiral fractures, inconsistent stories; mandated reporter

## NURSING PRIORITIES
- ABCs first
- Neutropenic fever: antibiotics immediately (within 1hr)
- Neurovascular: 5 P's
- ICP infant: bulging fontanel, high-pitched cry
- Post-op hemorrhage: frequent swallowing (tonsil), bright red vomitus
- Seizure: side-lying, protect, time, NO restraint
- Transfusion reaction: STOP immediately
- Epiglottitis: do NOT examine throat
- Meningitis: droplet precautions

## HIGH-YIELD
- Burns: phases, fluids (LR), urine output 1-2mL/kg/hr
- Croup vs Epiglottitis: barky cough vs drooling/tripod
- Celiac: gluten-free lifelong
- JIA: NSAIDs, DMARDs, eye exams
- Kawasaki: IVIG + aspirin
- Leukemia: neutropenic fever = emergency
- Sickle: hydration, avoid ice
- Hemophilia: factor VIII, ice, no NSAIDs
- Digoxin: hold if bradycardia
- Transfusion: STOP on reaction
- VP shunt: monitor malfunction signs
- Seizures: side-lying, NO mouth objects
- Meningitis: droplet, antibiotics after cultures
- Burns: reverse isolation, high-calorie nutrition
- DDH: Pavlik, asymmetric folds
- Scoliosis: Adams test, bracing
