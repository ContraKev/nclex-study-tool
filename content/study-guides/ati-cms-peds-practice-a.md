## 🔥 Burns & Trauma Care

### Phases of Burn Management

**Emergent/Resuscitative Phase (0-48hr):**
- Airway management, humidified O2
- Shock prevention: IV LR (Parkland formula)
- Intubation readiness
- Pain control (IV opioids before dressing changes)

**Acute Phase (2-5 days → wound closure):**
- Infection prevention: reverse isolation, no plants
- Wound care, nutrition (high protein/calories)
- Dark urine = myoglobinuria (electrical burns → renal risk)

**Rehabilitative Phase:**
- Scar prevention, contractures
- ROM exercises, pressure garments
- Psychosocial support

## 🫁 Respiratory Disorders

### Croup Syndromes

| Type | Cause | Signs | Treatment |
|------|-------|-------|----------|
| Acute laryngotracheobronchitis | RSV, parainfluenza | Barky cough, stridor, retractions | Cool mist, racemic epi, steroids |
| Epiglottitis | H. influenzae | Drooling, tripod, stridor | EMERGENCY — O2, airway, antibiotics |
| Spasmodic laryngitis | Allergens | Sudden stridor at night | Humidity, steroids |

**Key teaching:** Do NOT examine throat in epiglottitis — airway spasm risk.

### Asthma

- **Early signs:** Wheezes, prolonged expiration
- **LATE/EMERGENCY:** Silent chest = ventilatory failure → immediate intervention
- Triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air
- Meds: Albuterol (rescue), steroids (control)

### Bronchiolitis (RSV)

- Supportive care, hydration, O2 prn
- Contact precautions, hand hygiene
- High-risk: prematurity, cardiac disease

## 🍎 GI & Nutrition Disorders

### Key Disorders

| Disorder | Signs | Nursing |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Celiac Disease | Chronic diarrhea, FTT, distention | Strict gluten-free diet |
| Lactose Intolerance | Gas, cramps, osmotic diarrhea | Lactase supplements, avoid milk |
| Pyloric Stenosis | Projectile vomiting, olive mass | NPO preop, pyloromyotomy |
| Intussusception | Currant jelly stool, sausage mass | Air enema, surgery if needed |
| Appendicitis | RLQ pain, rebound tenderness | NPO, IV, surgery |
| Cleft Lip/Palate | Feeding difficulty | Special nipples, upright feed, elbow restraints postop |

**Feeding Principles:**
- Head elevated 30°+ for NG/GT feeds
- Check tube placement (pH 0-4 for gastric)
- ORS preferred over IV for mild-moderate dehydration

## 🦠 Infection Control

### Common Pediatric Infections

| Infection | Precaution | Key Points |
|-----------|------------|------------|
| Impetigo | Contact | Hot water wash linens, separate towels |
| Varicella | Airborne + Contact | Vesicular rash, contagious until crusts |
| RSV/Bronchiolitis | Contact | Hand hygiene, cohort |
| Meningitis | Droplet | Droplet precautions until 24hr antibiotics |

**Standard Precautions Always:** Hand hygiene, PPE as indicated.

## 🩸 Hematologic & Oncologic

### Sickle Cell Crisis

- **Priority:** Hydration, O2, pain control
- Avoid cold, dehydration, infection
- Transfusion for severe anemia or acute chest

### Hemophilia

- Factor replacement for bleeding
- RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation
- Avoid IM injections, aspirin/NSAIDs

### Leukemia

- Infection precautions (neutropenia)
- Bleeding precautions
- Chemo: monitor for tumor lysis, extravasation

### Acute Hemolytic Transfusion Reaction

- **Immediate:** Stop transfusion, maintain IV with NS
- Signs: Chills, fever, back pain, hypotension, hemoglobinuria
- Notify provider, collect blood samples

## 🦴 MSK & Neurologic

### Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)

- Morning stiffness, joint swelling
- ROM exercises, swimming, warm baths
- Uveitis screening (slit-lamp)

### Meningitis

- **Infants:** Bulging fontanelle, fever, irritability
- **Older:** Kernig/Brudzinski signs
- Droplet precautions, IV antibiotics

### Seizures

- Safety: Side-lying, protect head, loosen clothing
- Do NOT restrain or put anything in mouth
- Time seizure, document

## 🧠 Development & Age-Appropriate Care

### Teaching by Age

| Age | Approach |
|-----|----------|
| Toddler | Minimize physical contact initially; progress least→most traumatic |
| Preschool | Use sensory terms (what they'll see/feel/hear) |
| School-age | Use photos/diagrams, preop tour |
| Adolescent | Privacy, body image, involve in decisions |

### Pain Assessment

| Tool | Age | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| FLACC | Infants/nonverbal | Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability |
| FACES | 3-12 yr | Self-report with faces |
| Numeric | 8+ yr | 0-10 scale |

## 💊 Medication Administration

- **Always:** Weight-based dosing (mg/kg)
- Verify safe range
- Eardrops: Pull ear DOWN and BACK (<3yr), UP and BACK (>3yr)
- Eyedrops: Lower conjunctival sac
- Epinephrine auto-injector: Weight-based, expires, rotate sites
- Never crush enteric-coated or extended-release

## ✅ Priority Setting (ATI Style)

1. **ABCs** — Airway, Breathing, Circulation
2. **Safety** — Prevent harm first
3. **Least Restrictive** — Age-appropriate, family-centered
4. **Maslow** — Physiological before psychological
5. **Urgent vs Nonurgent** — Acute before chronic
