## 🤱 CHAPTER 6: LABOR & BIRTH

### The Four "P's" of Labor
| P | Meaning |
|---|---------|
| **Powers** | Uterine contractions + maternal pushing |
| **Passage** | Bony pelvis + soft tissues |
| **Passenger** | Fetus (size, presentation, position) |
| **Psyche** | Mental/emotional state of mother |

### Additional Factors (6 P's)
Preparation • Position • Professional • Place • Procedures • People

### Uterine Contractions
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Effacement** | Thinning/shortening of the cervical canal (0-100%) |
| **Dilation** | Opening of the cervical os (0-10 cm) |
| **Frequency** | Time from start of one contraction to start of next |
| **Duration** | Length of contraction (start → end) |
| **Intensity** | Strength: mild / moderate / firm |

### Three Phases of a Contraction
1. **Increment** (building)
2. **Peak/Acme** (strongest)
3. **Decrement** (letting up)

### Signs of Impending Labor
- Braxton Hicks → more regular
- **Bloody show** (mucus plug + blood)
- Rupture of membranes
- **Lightening/dropping** (baby descends)
- Energy spurt ("nesting")
- Weight loss, loose stools

### True vs. False Labor
| True Labor | False Labor |
|------------|-------------|
| Contractions **regular**, ↑ frequency/intensity | Irregular, no pattern |
| Continue despite position change | May stop with rest/position change |
| **Cervical change** (effacement/dilation) | No cervical change |
| Progressive | Non-progressive |

### When to Go to Hospital ⭐
**3-2-1 Rule** (primipara): Contractions **3 min apart, lasting 1 min, for 1 hour**
- Ruptured membranes
- Bleeding (other than bloody show)
- Decreased fetal movement
- Any concern

### Mechanisms (Cardinal Movements) of Labor
> **D → E → F → I → E → E → Ex**
> **Descent → Engagement → Flexion → Internal rotation → Extension → External rotation → Expulsion**

### Stages of Labor
| Stage | Name | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|------|----------|--------------|
| **First** | Dilation & Effacement | 4-6 hrs | Longest stage; 0-10 cm dilation |
| **Second** | Expulsion | 30 min - 2 hrs | Baby born |
| **Third** | Placenta | 5-30 min | Placenta delivered (Schultze/Duncan) |
| **Fourth** | Recovery | 1-2 hrs | Hemorrhage prevention, bonding |

### Fetal Heart Rate (FHR)
| Finding | Normal/Abnormal |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Baseline** | **110-160 bpm** |
| **Bradycardia** | <110 bpm |
| **Tachycardia** | >160 bpm |
| **Moderate variability** | ✅ Reassuring |
| **Absent/Minimal variability** | ⚠️ Concerning |
| **Accelerations** | ✅ Reassuring |

### FHR Decelerations — VEAL CHOP ⭐
| Deceleration | Cause | Intervention |
|--------------|-------|--------------|
| **V**ariable | **C**ord compression | Reposition, O2, amnioinfusion |
| **E**arly | **H**ead compression | None (normal) |
| **A**cceleration | **O**K (good!) | None |
| **L**ate | **P**lacental insufficiency | O2, reposition, stop Pitocin, notify MD |

### Amniotic Fluid Assessment
| Finding | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Clear + white flecks (vernix) | ✅ Normal |
| **Green/meconium** | ⚠️ Fetal distress, MAS risk |
| Foul odor | ⚠️ Infection |
| Scant vs. large | Assess amount |

### Apgar Score (at 1 and 5 min)
| Component | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| Heart rate | Absent | <100 | ≥100 |
| Respirations | Absent | Slow/irregular | Good cry |
| Muscle tone | Flaccid | Some flexion | Active motion |
| Reflex irritability | None | Grimace | Cry/cough |
| Color | Blue/pale | Body pink, extremities blue | All pink |

> **7-10** = Normal | **4-6** = Moderate distress | **0-3** = Severe distress

### Newborn Medications Immediately After Birth
| Medication | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| **Vitamin K (AquaMEPHYTON)** | Prevents hemorrhagic disease (vit K deficiency) |
| **Erythromycin eye ointment** | Prevents gonococcal/chlamydial ophthalmia neonatorum |

### Immediate Newborn Care (Phase 1: Birth → 1 hr)
- Maintain **thermoregulation** (warm, dry, skin-to-skin)
- Maintain **cardiorespiratory function**
- Observe for urination/meconium
- Identify mother/father/infant
- Brief anomaly assessment
- Encourage bonding/breastfeeding

### VBAC (Vaginal Birth After Cesarean)
- Main concern: **uterine scar rupture**
- Low transverse incision = best candidate
- Provide **extra emotional support**

### Immediate Postpartum (4th Stage)
- **Prevent hemorrhage**: fundal massage, assess lochia
- Monitor VS, bladder output
- Ice to perineum
- Keep warm and dry
- Promote bonding

## 💊 CHAPTER 7: PAIN MANAGEMENT IN LABOR

### Childbirth Preparation Methods
| Method | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| **Dick-Read** | Education reduces fear → reduces pain |
| **Bradley** | Husband-coached, natural childbirth |
| **Lamaze** | Breathing, relaxation, focal points |

### Nonpharmacologic Pain Relief
- **Effleurage** (light abdominal stroking)
- Breathing techniques (patterned, slow)
- Position changes, hydrotherapy, massage
- Visualization, music, aromatherapy
- **Doula/birth support**

⚠️ **Hyperventilation signs**: Dizziness, tingling hands/feet, muscle cramps, blurred vision
→ **Fix**: Breathe into cupped hands, slow exhalation

### Pharmacologic Pain Management

**Systemic Analgesics** (opioids)
- Given early in labor (not within 1 hr of birth)
- Can cause **neonatal respiratory depression**
- **Naloxone (Narcan)** = antidote

**Regional Anesthesia**
| Type | Block Area | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| **Epidural** | T10-S5 | Most common; continuous infusion |
| **Spinal (subarachnoid)** | Lower body | Single dose; rapid onset |
| **Pudendal** | Perineum | Vaginal birth, episiotomy |
| **Local infiltration** | Perineum | For episiotomy repair |

**General Anesthesia**
- Emergency C-section only
- Risk: **aspiration** → chemical lung injury
- Neonatal respiratory depression

### Pregnancy Physiology & Anesthesia
- ↑ Aspiration risk (sluggish GI)
- **Aortocaval compression** → hypotension (left lateral tilt)
- ↑ Hypoxia risk
- All medications affect **both mother and fetus**

## ⚠️ CHAPTER 8: LABOR & BIRTH COMPLICATIONS

### Induction vs. Augmentation
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Induction** | Starting labor artificially (before it begins) |
| **Augmentation** | Stimulating contractions that have already started |

### Bishop Score ⭐
> Assesses cervical readiness for induction
> **Score >6** = favorable for induction

Components: dilation, effacement, station, consistency, position

### Contraindications to Induction
- **Cephalopelvic disproportion (CPD)**
- **Placenta previa**
- **Active herpes infection**
- Fetal malpresentation, umbilical cord prolapsed, previous classical C-section

### Amniotomy (AROM)
> Artificial rupture of membranes
> Commits woman to delivery

**Complications**: Cord prolapse, infection, abruption

### Amnioinfusion
Warmed saline infused into uterus via IUPC
- **Indications**: Oligohydramnios, cord compression (variable decels), dilute meconium

### Perineal Lacerations
| Degree | Structures Involved |
|--------|---------------------|
| **1st** | Vaginal mucosa, perineal skin |
| **2nd** | + Deeper perineal tissues |
| **3rd** | + Anal sphincter |
| **4th** | + Rectal mucosa |

### Cesarean Birth
**Indications**: CPD, breech, placenta previa, fetal distress, failed induction, maternal disease, previous C-section

**Incision Types**:
| Incision | Location | VBAC? |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Low transverse** (Pfannenstiel) | Skin + uterine | ✅ Yes |
| Low vertical | Less common | ⚠️ Riskier |
| **Classic** | Upper uterine segment | ❌ No |

**Risks to mother**: Anesthesia, hemorrhage, blood clots, UTI, paralytic ileus, infection

### Dystocia (Difficult Labor)
Caused by abnormality of:
- **Power** (contractions)
- **Passenger** (fetus size, presentation, position)
- **Passage** (pelvis)
- **Psyche** (anxiety, lack of support)

### Abnormal Fetal Positions
- **Occiput posterior (OP)**: "Back labor" → **hands/knees, pelvic rocking**
- **Breech**: Buttocks or feet first
- Nursing: Encourage positions favoring rotation

### Preterm Labor ⭐
**Risk Factors**: Previous PTL, infection, substance abuse, multifetal, inadequate prenatal care, dehydration, smoking, DES exposure, chronic stress

**Symptoms**: Menstrual-like cramps, low backache, pelvic pressure, change in discharge, "feeling bad"

**Fetal Fibronectin (fFN) Test**: Detects protein in vaginal fluid → predicts PTL risk (22-24 weeks)

**Tocolytics** (stop contractions):
| Drug | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Magnesium sulfate** | Also neuroprotection; monitor RR (>12), DTRs; antidote = **calcium gluconate** |
| **Terbutaline** | Tachycardia, tremors, hyperglycemia |
| **Nifedipine** | Calcium channel blocker |

**Betamethasone**: Given **48 hrs before** delivery (2 doses, 24 hrs apart) → accelerates fetal lung maturity

### Obstetric Emergencies

**Prolapsed Umbilical Cord**
- Cord slips below presenting part → compresses → fetal hypoxia
- **Immediate action**: **Knee-chest position**, push presenting part OFF cord, call for help, prepare for C-section
- May ride on stretcher to OR with hand in place

**Uterine Rupture**
- Complete (through all layers), incomplete, dehiscence
- Risk: prior C-section, trauma, hyperstimulation
- Signs: sudden pain, hemorrhage, loss of contractions, fetal distress

**Amniotic Fluid Embolism (Anaphylactoid Syndrome)**
- Amniotic fluid enters maternal circulation
- **Life-threatening**: respiratory arrest, shock, DIC
- Tx: respiratory support, fluids, coagulation factors
