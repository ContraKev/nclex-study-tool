## 📚 Developmental Theories

### Erikson's Psychosocial Development

| Age | Stage | Positive Resolution | Negative | Nursing |
|-----|-------|---------------------|----------|---------|
| 0-1 | Trust vs Mistrust | Trust, hope | Mistrust, fear | Consistent care, respond to cries |
| 1-3 | Autonomy vs Shame | Independence | Shame, doubt | Offer choices, support toilet training |
| 3-6 | Initiative vs Guilt | Purpose | Guilt | Encourage play, don't shame curiosity |
| 6-12 | Industry vs Inferiority | Competence | Inferiority | Support school achievement |

### Piaget's Cognitive Development

| Stage | Age | Key | Nursing |
|-------|-----|-----|---------|
| Sensorimotor | 0-2 | Object permanence | Provide sensory play |
| Preoperational | 2-7 | Egocentric, magical thinking | Use simple words, show pictures |
| Concrete Operational | 7-11 | Logical, conservation | Use concrete examples, hands-on |
| Formal Operational | 11+ | Abstract | Discuss concepts, future planning |

### Kohlberg's Moral Development

| Level | Age | Focus |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Preconventional | <9 | Punishment/reward |
| Conventional | 9-13 | Social approval, rules |
| Postconventional | 13+ | Justice, ethical principles |

### Nutrition
- Breast milk or formula only for first 6 months
- Introduce solids at 6 months (iron-fortified cereal first)
- No honey until 12 months (botulism risk)
- No cow's milk until 12 months

### Milestones
| Age | Milestone |
|-----|-----------|
| 2 mo | Social smile, lifts head prone |
| 4 mo | Rolls over, reaches for objects |
| 6 mo | Sits alone, stranger anxiety |
| 9 mo | Crawls, pincer grasp, mama/dada |
| 12 mo | Walks with help, 2-3 words |

### Nursing Considerations
- Use growth charts (plot weight, height, head circumference)
- Denver Developmental Screening Test at well visits
- Support breastfeeding; teach proper latch and positioning
- Safety: rear-facing car seat, no soft bedding (SIDS prevention)
- Immunizations per schedule

## 🧸 Chapter 17: TODDLER (1-3 years)

### Psychosocial Development
- **Erikson:** Autonomy vs Shame/Doubt
- "No!" phase is normal — child asserting independence
- Toilet training readiness: 18-24 months

### Cognitive
- Piaget: Preoperational — magical thinking, egocentrism, symbolic play

### Nutrition
- Food jags (only wants one food) are normal
- Offer choices: "apple or banana?"
- Limit juice to 4-6 oz/day
- 16-24 oz milk/day max (too much causes iron deficiency)

### Safety
- Car seat: rear-facing until age 2
- Poison prevention: lock cabinets, keep chemicals out of reach
- Fall prevention: gates on stairs
- Drowning: never leave unattended near water

## 👦 Chapter 18: PRESCHOOL (3-5 years)

### Psychosocial
- **Erikson:** Initiative vs Guilt
- Takes initiative in activities
- Imaginary friends are normal

### Cognitive
- Piaget: Preoperational continues
- Animism (objects have feelings)
- Centration (focus on one aspect)

### Moral
- Kohlberg: Preconventional (punishment/reward)

### Health & Safety
- Bike helmet, seat belt
- Stranger danger education
- Screen time limits (2 hr/day max)
- Dental: brush after meals, floss

---

## 🏫 Chapter 19: SCHOOL-AGE (6-12 years)

### Psychosocial
- **Erikson:** Industry vs Inferiority
- Sense of competence from school achievement
- Peer relationships critical

### Cognitive
- Piaget: Concrete Operational
- Logical thinking about concrete objects
- Conservation, classification, seriation

### Health & Safety
- Bike helmet, seat belt
- Stranger danger, internet safety
- Dental: regular checkups, brush/floss
- Exercise: 60 min/day

## ✅ Quick Reference — High-Yield

### Growth & Development
- Weight doubles by 6 months, triples by 1 year
- Denver II screens 4 areas: personal-social, fine motor, language, gross motor
- Growth charts: percentile trends matter more than single values

### Nutrition Milestones
- 0-6 mo: breast milk or formula only
- 6 mo: introduce solids (cereal first)
- 12 mo: whole milk, finger foods

### Safety by Age
- Infant: SIDS prevention, car seat rear-facing
- Toddler: poisons, drowning, burns
- Preschool: bike helmet, stranger danger
- School-age: helmet, seatbelt, internet
