# Study Guide: Drugs for Cardiovascular Disorders & Diabetes Mellitus

## Part 1: Cardiovascular Disorders

### 1. Antihypertensives & Heart Failure

#### A. ACE Inhibitors ("-pril")
*   **Examples:** Lisinopril, Enalapril.
*   **Key Facts:** Dry cough (bradykinin), **Angioedema** (emergency), **Hyperkalemia**.
*   **Education:** Avoid potassium salt substitutes.

#### B. ARBs ("-sartan")
*   **Examples:** Losartan, Valsartan.
*   **Key Fact:** No cough. **Avoid in pregnancy**.

#### C. Calcium Channel Blockers
*   **Dihydropyridines (Amlodipine):** Peripheral edema (swollen ankles).
*   **Non-dihydropyridines (Verapamil, Diltiazem):** **Constipation**, Bradycardia.
*   **Interaction:** **Grapefruit juice** (toxic levels).

#### D. Beta Blockers ("-olol")
*   **Nursing:** Hold if HR < 60. **Masks hypoglycemia tachycardia**.
*   **Caution:** Bronchospasm in Asthma (Non-selective: Propranolol).

#### E. Diuretics
*   **Loop (Furosemide):** **Ototoxicity** (tinnitus), Hypokalemia.
*   **K-Sparing (Spironolactone):** **Hyperkalemia**. Avoid K+ rich foods.

#### F. Cardiac Glycoside: Digoxin
*   **Toxicity:** Yellow-green halos, nausea, bradycardia.
*   **Check:** Apical pulse 1 min (hold if <60). **Hypokalemia increases toxicity risk**.

### 2. Angina & Anticoagulants

#### A. Nitrates (Nitroglycerine, Isosorbide)
*   **Mechanism:** Potent vasodilator (venous and arterial).
*   **Side Effects:** Headache (common), Hypotension.
*   **Tolerance:** Must have **8-12 hour nitrate-free period** (e.g., take patch off at night) to prevent tolerance.

#### B. Anticoagulants & Antiplatelets
*   **Heparin:** Monitor **aPTT**. **Antidote: Protamine Sulfate**. Watch for HIT (low platelets).
*   **Enoxaparin (Lovenox):** Low molecular weight heparin. SQ only.
*   **Warfarin (Coumadin):** Monitor **PT/INR**. **Antidote: Vitamin K**. Maintain consistent green leafy veg intake.
*   **DOACs (Apixaban/Rivaroxaban):** No regular lab monitoring needed.
*   **Antiplatelets:** **Aspirin** (max 4g/day) and **Clopidogrel**. Used for PAD/CAD. Watch for bleeding.

---

### 3. Antihyperlipidemics (Statins & Others)

*   **Statins:** **Rhabdomyolysis** (Muscle pain + Tea-colored urine). **Evening dosing**. No grapefruit juice.
*   **Fibrates (Gemfibrozil):** Lowers triglycerides. Risk of **gallstones**.
*   **Bile Acid Resins:** Binds other meds. Take meds 1h before or 4-6h after.
*   **Niacin:** **Flushing**. Take Aspirin 30 mins before.

## Part 2: Diabetes Mellitus

### 1. Insulins

| Type | Onset | Peak | Duration | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rapid** | 15-30m | 0.5-2.5h | 3-6h | Lispro, Aspart |
| **Short** | 30-60m | 1-5h | 6-10h | **Regular (Only one for IV)** |
| **Intermediate** | 1-2h | 6-14h | 16-24h | NPH (Cloudy) |
| **Long** | 70m | **None** | 24h | Glargine, Detemir |

*   **Mixing:** **Clear (Regular) before Cloudy (NPH)**. Never mix Long-acting (Glargine).
*   **Education:** Rotate sites (**Lipohypertrophy**). Do NOT massage site. Exercise speeds absorption.

---

### 2. Oral & Injectable Antidiabetics

*   **Metformin:** **Hold 48h before/after IV Contrast**. GI upset (take with food). B12/Folate deficiency.
*   **Sulfonylureas (Glipizide):** **Hypoglycemia** if meal is skipped. Avoid Alcohol (Disulfiram-like reaction).
*   **TZDs (Pioglitazone):** **Fluid retention**. **Contraindicated in Heart Failure**.
*   **Alpha-glucosidase (Acarbose):** Slows carb absorption. **GI effects (bloating/gas)**. Anemia.
*   **SGLT2 (Canagliflozin):** **UTIs/Yeast infections**. Hypotension.
*   **Gliptins/GLP-1s:** **Pancreatitis** (severe back/abdominal pain).

---

### 3. Hypoglycemia (15/15 Rule)

*   **BG < 70:** 15g simple carbs (juice). Wait 15 mins. Repeat if still low.
*   **BG < 55 (Severe/Unconscious):** **Glucagon IM/SQ** or D50W IV.
*   **Recovery:** Give complex carb + protein (cheese and crackers).
