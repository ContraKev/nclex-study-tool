## 📊 CHAPTER 15: OVERVIEW OF GROWTH, DEVELOPMENT, AND NUTRITION

### Growth vs. Development
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Growth** | Measurable changes (height, weight, head circumference) |
| **Development** | Qualitative changes in skills, behaviors, cognition |

- Growth spurts followed by plateaus
- Not all parts mature at same time
- Children differ from adults in anatomy/physiology — illnesses present differently

### Developmental Theories

#### Erikson's Psychosocial Stages
| Stage | Age | Crisis | Key Task |
|-------|-----|--------|----------|
| Trust vs. Mistrust | 0-1 yr | Trust | Consistent care builds trust |
| Autonomy vs. Shame/Doubt | 1-3 yr | Autonomy | Independence, toilet training |
| Initiative vs. Guilt | 3-6 yr | Initiative | Purposeful play, curiosity |
| Industry vs. Inferiority | 6-12 yr | Industry | Competence, school achievement |

#### Piaget's Cognitive Stages
| Stage | Age | Key Feature |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **Sensorimotor** | 0-2 yr | Object permanence, reflexes → intentional actions |
| **Preoperational** | 2-7 yr | Egocentrism, magical thinking, symbolic play |
| **Concrete Operational** | 7-11 yr | Logical thinking, conservation, classification |
| **Formal Operational** | 11+ yr | Abstract reasoning, hypothetical thinking |

#### Kohlberg's Moral Development
| Level | Age | Focus |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Preconventional | <9 yr | Punishment/reward |
| Conventional | 9-13 yr | Social approval, rules |
| Postconventional | 13+ yr | Abstract principles, justice |

### Denver Developmental Screening Test
- Assesses: Personal-social, Fine motor-adaptive, Language, Gross motor
- Purpose: Identify children NOT performing at age-appropriate level
- NOT an intelligence test

### Nutrition Basics
- **Newborn GI**: Immature first 3 months — saliva minimal, relies on HCl/rennin/trypsin
- Fat/cholesterol NOT restricted in infants (needed for CNS development)
- **Childhood Obesity**: Rising post-pandemic — screen BMI, counsel family

### Dental Health
- First tooth erupts ~6 months
- **Baby bottle caries**: Milk/juice pools in mouth during sleep — most common 18mo-3yr
- **Education**: Brush after each meal/snack, replace brush every 3 months
- Sticky foods (gum, dried fruit, cake, candy) → more caries than sugared drinks
- Recommended snacks: cheese, milk, raw vegetables, sugarless gum
- **Teething**: Fever NOT associated — assess for other causes

### Play
- Play is the "work" of childhood — essential for development
- Types: Solitary → Parallel → Cooperative (age-dependent)

## 👶 CHAPTER 16: THE INFANT (0-12 MONTHS)

### Milestones by Age
| Age | Key Milestones |
|-----|----------------|
| **1-3 mo** | Social smile, lifts head prone, follows objects |
| **2-6 mo** | Rolls over, reaches for objects, sits with support |
| **3-10 mo** | Sits alone (6mo), pincer grasp developing, stranger anxiety |
| **10-12 mo** | Pulls to stand, cruises, says "mama/dada", walks with assistance |

> ⚠️ **6-month checkup**: If child CANNOT sit independently → developmental concern
> ⚠️ **10-month**: Should walk when led/held — if not, assess motor delay

### Growth Parameters
| Milestone | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| Birth weight | 6.6 lb avg |
| Doubles | 6 months (13.2 lb) |
| Triples | 1 year (19.8 lb) |
| Head = Chest circumference | By 1 year |
| Posterior fontanelle closes | ~2-3 months |
| Anterior fontanelle closes | ~12-18 months |

### Freud: Oral Stage
- Sucking = comfort and tension relief
- **Must**: Hold infant during feedings, allow sufficient sucking time
- Infants on IV/TPN need pacifier to satisfy sucking need
- Grasp reflex disappears ~3 months; pincer grasp established by 12 months

### Emotional Development
- **Consistency** = builds trust
- Infants consistently picked up when crying → fewer crying episodes, less aggressive behavior as toddlers
- Do NOT force skills before readiness — wait for cues

### Sleep Safety (SIDS Prevention)
- Firm mattress, no stuffed animals, no pillows
- Back to sleep ONLY
- No co-sleeping with adults
- Room-sharing (not bed-sharing) recommended

### Colic & Irritable Infant
- Excessive crying, often evenings, peaks 6-8 weeks
- **Coping strategies**: Swaddle, white noise, rhythmic motion, hold upright
- Rule out: hunger, wet diaper, illness

### Lethargic Infant
- May "shut down" from over-stimulation
- Interventions: Dim lights, gentle handling, calm voice, upright positioning

### Positive Sleep Patterns
- Newborns: 4-hour intervals
- By 4-6 months: Can sleep 8+ hours
- Teach self-soothing; avoid picking up immediately for every whimper
- **Circadian rhythm**: Synchronize to family routine

### Nutrition — Breastfeeding & Formula
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Contraindications to breastfeeding** | HIV, HTLV-1/2 (absolute) |
| **First foods** | Rice cereal at 6 months |
| **New food rule** | One food at a time; wait 3-5 days between |
| **Avoid early** | Orange juice, fish, nuts, strawberries, chocolate, egg whites (allergy risk) |
| **Adequate intake signs** | 4-7 oz/wk weight gain (first 6mo), ≥6 wet diapers/day, peaceful sleep after feeds |

### Immunizations
- Stress timing — delays = ↑ risk of serious illness/death
- Keep accurate records
- **4-month vaccines**: DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV, Rotavirus

### Safety
- **NEVER** put furniture next to window — fall hazard
- Car seat rear-facing until 2 years
- No hot liquids near infant
- Choking: Cut food small; no grapes, nuts, hot dogs (until toddler age)

## 🧒 CHAPTER 17: THE TODDLER (1-3 YEARS)

### Physical Development
| Change | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| Birth weight quadruples | 2.5 years |
| Bowel/bladder control | 2.5-3 years |
| Abdomen flattens | Muscle tone increases |
| Head/trunk growth slows | Body proportions change |

### Erikson: Autonomy vs. Shame & Doubt
- "No!" phase — normal assertion of independence
- **Temper tantrums**: **IGNORE** (do not reinforce with attention)
- Time-outs: 1 minute per year of age

### Toilet Training
- Voluntary sphincter control begins ~18-24 months
- Readiness signs: Dry for 2+ hours, recognizes urge, follows simple directions
- **Do NOT punish accidents** — positive reinforcement

### Speech & Language
| Age | Expected |
|-----|----------|
| 12 months | 2-3 words |
| 18 months | 10-20 words |
| 24 months | 2-word phrases |

> 🚩 **Autism Screening Red Flags**:
> - No pointing/gesturing by 12 months
> - No single words by 16 months
> - No two-word phrases by 24 months
> - Loss of previously achieved language/social skills

### Nutrition
- **Picky eater** vs. **Feeding disorder**:
  - Picky: Selective but maintains growth
  - Disorder: Refuses to eat → weight loss, developmental delays
- **Finger foods**: Appropriate; encourage self-feeding
- **Choking hazards**: Grapes, hot dogs, watermelon seeds, marshmallows, nuts
- Small portions; don't force eating

### Discipline & Guidance
- **Goal**: Teach, not punish
- Consistency is KEY
- Limit-setting must match child's abilities
- Praise positive behaviors

### Injury Prevention
- Childproof caps on medicines
- Smoke/CO detectors
- Water heater max temp ≤120°F
- Crib slats ≤2.375 inches apart
- Car seat: Rear-facing until age 2

## 🧸 CHAPTER 18: THE PRESCHOOL CHILD (3-5 YEARS)

### General Characteristics
- **Slowing of physical growth**
- Major tasks: Prepare for school, cooperative play, body control, separation acceptance
- Vocabulary explodes; memory and attention span increase

### Piaget: Preoperational Phase
| Substage | Age | Characteristics |
|----------|-----|-----------------|
| Preconceptual | 2-4 yr | Magical thinking, animism |
| Intuitive | 4-7 yr | Centration, egocentrism, irreversibility |

### Language Development
- Delays may be: Physiological, Psychological, Environmental
- Assess both receptive (understanding) and expressive language

### Bedtime Habits
- Quiet activities before bed
- Establish rituals (story, song, bath)
- Do NOT reinforce attention-getting by allowing into parent's bed

### Discipline
- **Time-out**: 1 minute per year of age
- No interaction or eye contact during timeout
- **Reward** ≠ **Bribe** — reward reinforces positive behavior
- Consistency and modeling are critical

### Thumb Sucking
- Not harmful if discontinued before permanent teeth erupt (~6 years)
- Praise and encourage when trying to stop
- May regress during stress or fatigue

### Enuresis (Bedwetting)
| Primary | Secondary |
|---------|-----------|
| Never achieved dryness | Had dryness ≥6 months then regressed |

**Organic causes to rule out**: UTI, DM, DI, seizures, obstructive uropathy, sleep disorders

### Play in Illness
- **Therapeutic play**: Helps child express feelings, understand procedures
- **Art therapy**: Nonverbal expression
- Play should be noncompetitive
- Consider mental age (not chronological) for neurodevelopmental disorders

## 📚 CHAPTER 19: THE SCHOOL-AGE CHILD (6-12 YEARS)

### Erikson: Industry vs. Inferiority
- Success in school, sports, peer relationships builds competence
- Failure → feelings of inferiority

### Freud: Latency Stage
- Sexual drives are dormant; focus on friendships, learning

### Physical Growth
- Slows until pre-puberty (growth spurt 9-12)
- Brain reaches adult size
- Lower center of gravity → improved coordination
- Size ≠ emotional maturity — don't raise expectations based on height

### Age-Specific Development

| Age | Key Characteristics |
|-----|---------------------|
| **6 yr** | Energetic, starts tasks (may not finish), 2500-word vocab, 11-13 hrs sleep |
| **7 yr** | High standards, good humor, modest, enjoys rest periods |
| **8 yr** | Independent play, creative, hero-worship, behaves better for company |
| **9 yr** | Dependable, responsible, completes tasks, worries common |
| **10 yr** | Preadolescence begins, girls more mature than boys, group > individual |
| **11-12 yr** | Intense, argumentative, hormone influence apparent, need freedom within limits |

### Sex Education
- Lifelong process — mostly modeled, not just "talked about"
- Answer questions simply; use correct anatomical terms
- Private masturbation is normal
- STIs: Begin education before puberty

### School-Related Tasks
- Set realistic goals
- Monitor attendance, tardiness, mood changes
- Homework = child's responsibility
- Allowance teaches money value

### Latchkey Children
- Children who return home alone after school
- Teach: Safety rules, emergency contacts, not to answer door, kitchen safety

### Pet Ownership
- Close contact pets can transmit disease
- Children with disabilities benefit greatly from pets
- Teach: No kissing pets, no sleeping in bed with pets, hand hygiene

## 🔬 COMMON PEDIATRIC CONDITIONS (from class notes)

### Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia (ALL)
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **What** | Blood & bone marrow cancer — immature lymphocytes |
| **Symptoms** | Fatigue, bleeding/bruising, bone pain, frequent infections |
| **Oral care** | Soft sponge toothbrush; **NO viscous lidocaine** (toxic in mouth ulcers) |
| **Mouth ulcers** | Aluminum hydroxide + Mg hydroxide + diphenhydramine (magic mouthwash) |
| **Steroids** | Oral steroids (immunosuppressed) |
| **Vaccines** | **Cannot receive live vaccines** (chickenpox, MMR) |

### Cystic Fibrosis
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Diagnosis** | Sweat chloride test (elevated NaCl) |
| **Treatment** | Chest PT, postural drainage |
| **Nutrition** | Unrestricted salt, high calorie, high protein |
| **Genetic** | Autosomal recessive — both parents carriers |

### Pain Scales
| Scale | Age | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **FLACC** | Infants | Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability |
| **CRIES** | Neonates | Crying, Requires O2, Increased VS, Expression, Sleepless |
| **FACES** | 3-7 yr | Most effective ≥4yr; recommended by 3yr |
| **Numeric** | ≥8 yr | Standard 0-10 scale |
| **Babinski** | Birth-12mo | Fan toes on stroking sole — normal until 12mo |

## 🏥 MAINTENANCE OF THE HOSPITALIZED CHILD

### Major Stressors by Age Group
| Age | Primary Stressor | Nursing Interventions |
|-----|------------------|-----------------------|
| **Infant** | Stranger anxiety, separation from parents | Consistent caregivers, parental presence, transitional objects |
| **Toddler** | Loss of control, routine disruption | Familiar objects, choices when possible, maintain rituals |
| **Preschool** | Fear of bodily injury/mutilation, magical thinking | Prepare with concrete explanations, use dolls/toys to demonstrate |
| **School-age** | Loss of control, privacy, separation from peers | Allow choices, privacy, maintain school contact |
| **Adolescent** | Threat to independence, body image, peer relationships | Privacy, involve in decisions, peer contact |

### Separation Anxiety Phases
| Phase | Behavior | Nursing Response |
|-------|----------|------------------|
| **Protest** | Cries, clings, rejects strangers | Encourage parental presence |
| **Despair** | Quiet, withdrawn, sad | Provide comfort, involve parents |
| **Detachment** | Appears "adjusted" but apathetic | Recognize as coping; keep parents involved |

### Atraumatic Care Principles
- Prepare child in advance using age-appropriate language
- Use therapeutic play / child-life specialists
- Involve family as constant presence
- Minimize pain and procedures when possible
- Promote coping through choices and control

### Family-Centered Care
- Family is the child's constant — involve them in all care
- Sibling visits may help reduce separation anxiety
- Teach parents what to expect; provide written instructions
- Encourage parents to participate in care (feeding, bathing)

### Pain Assessment & Management
- **FLACC** scale for infants/toddlers (Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability)
- **FACES** scale for 3+ years
- **Numeric** (0-10) for 8+ years
- Nonpharmacologic: distraction, music, comfort positioning, sucrose for infants
- Pharmacologic: weight-based dosing; acetaminophen/ibuprofen first line

### Hospital Play & Therapeutic Play
- **Medical play**: Child plays with equipment (stethoscope, syringe without needle)
- **Dramatic play**: Acting out procedures on dolls
- **Art/play therapy**: Expresses feelings nonverbally
- Play should be **noncompetitive** for ill children

### Communication Tips
- Use concrete, simple terms (preschoolers take words literally)
- Allow time for silence — especially culturally (e.g., Native American children)
- Speak at eye level; use play for demonstration
- Explain "why" to school-age/adolescents — they understand reasoning

### Additional Key Points
- **Ordinal position** (birth order) influences development — youngest may have less need to communicate independently
- **Cephalocaudal** = head-to-toe development; **Proximodistal** = center-to-periphery
- **Blended family** = parents have children from previous relationships + together
- **Cooperative play** = group play with assigned roles (preschool age)
- **Push-pull toys** appropriate for 10-month infant; model airport for 5-year-old
- **Picky eater** = selective but growth maintained; **Feeding disorder** = refusal → weight loss
