## 🧬 FETAL DEVELOPMENT (Ch. 3)

### Cell Division
| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Meiosis** | Reduces chromosomes to **23** (sex cells) |
| **Mitosis** | Copies all **46** chromosomes (body growth) |

### Fertilization → Implantation Timeline
| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|-------------|
| **Fertilization** | Sperm + ovum unite in **outer third of fallopian tube** |
| **Zygote** | Single fertilized cell |
| **Morula** | Solid ball of 16-32 cells; enters uterus by **day 3** |
| **Blastocyst** | Floats 2-4 more days, then **implants** (upper posterior uterine wall) |
| **Decidua** | Endometrium after implantation; **decidua basalis** = maternal part of placenta |

> 🔑 Sperm survive **5 days**, ovum survives **24 hours**. Sex determined at fertilization (XX or XY).

### Three Germ Layers
| Layer | Develops Into | Memory Aid |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Ectoderm** (outer) | Skin, hair, nails, nervous system, sense organs, mouth/anus lining | **E**cto = **E**xterior |
| **Mesoderm** (middle) | Skeleton, muscles, blood vessels, kidneys, gonads | **M**eso = **M**uscle |
| **Endoderm** (inner) | Trachea, bronchi, GI tract lining, bladder, urethra | **En**do = **In**ternal linings |

### Key Milestones
- **8 weeks**: All body systems formed, human appearance, enters **fetal period**
- **20 weeks**: **Age of viability** (textbook); clinical viability ~22-24 weeks
- **37 weeks**: Term (obstetrical); **39-40 weeks** = full term
- **Amniotic fluid**: 30 mL (10wk) → 350 mL (20wk) → **1000 mL (37wk)**

### Placenta — "Temporary Endocrine Organ"
**Four Hormones:**
| Hormone | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Progesterone** | Maintains uterine lining, ↓ contractions, preps breasts for lactation |
| **Estrogen** | ↑ Uterine growth, ↑ blood flow, breast duct development |
| **hCG** | Signals conception, maintains corpus luteum; detected by pregnancy tests (7-9 days post-fertilization) |
| **hPL** | ↓ Maternal insulin sensitivity → more glucose for fetus ("diabetogenic") |

### Umbilical Cord
> **2 arteries, 1 vein** — covered by **Wharton jelly** — ~55 cm (22 in)
> - **Arteries** = waste AWAY from fetus
> - **Vein** = oxygenated blood TO fetus

### Three Fetal Shunts ⭐
| Shunt | Bypasses | Closes After Birth |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Ductus venosus** | Liver | When cord cut (permanent ~1 week) |
| **Foramen ovale** | Lungs (R→L atrium) | **2 hours** (permanent ~3 months) |
| **Ductus arteriosus** | Lungs (pulm artery→aorta) | **15 hours** (permanent ~3 weeks) |

> After permanent closure → become **ligaments**

### Twins
- **Monozygotic** = identical, 1 egg, shared chorion/placenta
- **Dizygotic** = fraternal, 2 eggs + 2 sperm, separate placentas (may fuse)

### Teratogens
Agents causing embryo malformation: medications, smoking, alcohol, undernutrition, infections

## 🤰 PRENATAL CARE & ADAPTATIONS (Ch. 4)

### Gravida/Para Terminology
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Gravida** | # of pregnancies |
| **Para** | # of births ≥20 weeks (alive or still) |
| **GTPAL** | Gravida, Term, Preterm, Abortions, Living |
| **Nullipara** | Never given birth past 20 weeks |
| **Multipara** | 2+ births past 20 weeks |

### Nägele's Rule (EDD Calculation) ⭐
> **LMP − 3 months + 7 days**
> Example: LMP May 5 → EDD = **February 12**

### Signs of Pregnancy

| Presumptive (subjective) | Probable (objective) | Positive (definitive) |
|-------------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Amenorrhea | Goodell sign (cervix softens) | Fetal heartbeat heard |
| Nausea/vomiting | Chadwick sign (purplish cervix) | Fetal movement felt by examiner |
| Breast tenderness | Hegar sign (lower uterus softens) | Fetus visible on ultrasound |
| Fatigue, urinary frequency | Positive pregnancy test (hCG) | |
| Quickening (mom feels movement) | Braxton Hicks contractions | |
| Deepening pigmentation | Ballottement, abdominal enlargement | |

### Routine Prenatal Labs by Trimester
**1st Trimester**: Blood type/Rh, antibody screen, CBC, RPR (syphilis), rubella titer, TB screen, Hep B, HIV, UA/culture, Pap, chlamydia/gonorrhea, HbA1C, genetic screening (NIPT)

**2nd Trimester**: Quad screen, anatomy ultrasound, glucose challenge test (GDM screening 24-28wk)

**3rd Trimester**: GBS culture (35-37wk), repeat STI/HIV if indicated, NST/BPP, cervical fetal fibronectin

### Every Prenatal Visit
Vital signs + weight, urinalysis (glucose/protein), fundal height, FHR, Leopold maneuvers (late pregnancy), risk factors, nutrition, discomforts

### Four Critical Nutrients in Pregnancy
| Nutrient | Daily Need | Key Points |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Protein** | 60 g/day | Meat, fish, poultry, dairy |
| **Calcium** | 1000 mg | Dairy, leafy greens; needs Vit D |
| **Iron** | 30 mg/day | 2x non-pregnant; prevents anemia |
| **Folic acid** | 400 mcg daily | Prevents **neural tube defects** (B vitamin) |

> 💊 **Ferrous sulfate (iron)**: Take with **orange juice** (vitamin C enhances absorption). Causes dark stools and constipation. Do NOT take with milk, antacids, or calcium.
> ⚠️ **Excess Vitamin A = teratogenic** (fetal anomalies/cardiac defects)
> 💡 **Vitamin B6 + ginger** = recommended for pregnancy nausea
> 🚫 **PICA** = craving/eating non-food substances (clay, starch, ice)

### Normal Weight Gain: ~25-35 lbs (normal BMI)

### Fundal Height Landmarks ⭐
- At **20 weeks**: fundus at **umbilicus**
- **After delivery**: fundus at umbilicus, then descends 1-2 cm/day
- Fundal height (in cm) ≈ gestational age (weeks) from 20-36 weeks

### Pregnancy Blood Values (vs. Non-Pregnant)
| Lab | Pregnant | Non-Pregnant |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Hgb | **11-12** g/dL | 12-16 |
| Hct | 33-46% | 36-48% |
| WBC | **5,000-15,000** | 5,000-10,000 |
| Fibrinogen | **300-600** mg/dL | 200-400 |

### Supine Hypotension Syndrome
> Uterus compresses **inferior vena cava** when lying flat → ↓ BP, dizziness
> **Fix: Turn to LEFT SIDE**

### Exercise: OK vs. Avoid
✅ Walking, swimming, low-impact
🚫 Hot tubs, saunas, scuba, skiing, horseback riding, contact sports

### Travel Safety
- Seatbelt: **lap belt UNDER belly**, shoulder strap between breasts
- Frequent breaks, hydration, ROM exercises (DVT prevention)

### Common Discomforts & Remedies
| Discomfort | Interventions |
|-----------|--------------|
| Nausea | Small frequent meals, crackers before rising, B6 + ginger |
| Heartburn | Small meals, avoid lying down after eating, antacids if approved |
| Constipation | Fluids, fiber, exercise (normal in pregnancy — progesterone slows GI) |
| Leg cramps | Dorsiflex foot, calcium/magnesium |
| Fatigue | Rest, balanced nutrition |
| **Lordosis** (low back pain) | Caused by shifting center of gravity; pelvic tilts, supportive shoes |
| **Leukorrhea** | Increased vaginal discharge — **normal** during pregnancy |
| **Urinary frequency** | Normal in **1st and 3rd trimesters** (uterine pressure on bladder) |

### Key Pregnancy Tests ⭐
| Test | What It Detects | When |
|------|----------------|------|
| **AFP (alpha-fetoprotein)** | **Neural tube defects** (elevated) / Down syndrome (low) | 2nd trimester (part of quad screen) |
| **Amniocentesis** | Genetic disorders, lung maturity | US-guided to **avoid fetus and placenta** |
| **GBS culture** | Group B Strep colonization | **35-37 weeks** |
| **Glucose challenge** | Gestational diabetes | **24-28 weeks** |

### Key Medications in Pregnancy ⭐
| Medication | Use | Key Nursing Points |
|-----------|-----|-------------------|
| **RhoGAM** | Rh incompatibility prevention | Given at 28 wk + within 72 hrs postpartum if baby Rh+ |
| **Magnesium sulfate** | Seizure prevention (preeclampsia) + tocolytic | **Monitor respiratory rate** (hold if RR <12); check DTRs; antidote = **calcium gluconate** |
| **Terbutaline** | Tocolytic (stops preterm contractions) | Side effects: **tachycardia, tremors, hyperglycemia, hypokalemia**; monitor FHR and maternal HR |
| **Betamethasone** | Fetal lung maturity | Give **48 hrs before** anticipated preterm delivery; 2 doses 24 hrs apart |
| **Rubella vaccine** | MMR — prevent rubella | **NEVER during pregnancy** (live vaccine); give **after delivery** |
| **Ferrous sulfate** | Iron supplementation | Take with **OJ** (vitamin C); avoid milk/antacids; causes dark stools |

### Quickening & Smoking
- **Quickening**: first fetal movement felt by mother at **4-5 months** (16-20 weeks)
- **Smoking**: increases risk of **preterm birth** and **low birth weight**

### FDA Drug Categories in Pregnancy
**A** (safe) → **B** → **C** → **D** (evidence of risk) → **X** (contraindicated)

## ⚠️ HIGH-RISK PREGNANCY (Ch. 5) — Key Concepts

### Danger Signs in Pregnancy — REPORT IMMEDIATELY
- Sudden gush of fluid, vaginal bleeding, abdominal pain
- Persistent vomiting, epigastric pain
- Facial/hand edema, severe headache, blurred vision
- Chills/fever >100.4°F, dysuria or ↓ urine output

### Hyperemesis Gravidarum
Excessive N/V → dehydration → ↓ placental perfusion → >5% weight loss, electrolyte imbalance, ketonuria

### Hyperemesis vs. Normal Morning Sickness
| Normal N/V | Hyperemesis Gravidarum |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Mild, manageable | Excessive, persistent |
| No weight loss | **>5% weight loss** |
| Adequate hydration | Dehydration, ketonuria, electrolyte imbalance |

### Types of Miscarriage/Abortion
| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Threatened** | Bleeding but cervix closed; pregnancy may continue |
| **Inevitable** | Cervix dilating; pregnancy cannot be saved |
| **Incomplete** | Some tissue passed, some retained |
| **Complete** | All products of conception expelled |
| **Missed** | Fetus dies but is retained |

> Post-miscarriage teaching: Report increased bleeding, no tampons, temp q8h x 3 days, report fever ≥100.4°F, no sex until cleared

### Ectopic Pregnancy
Implantation outside uterus (usually fallopian tube). **Rupture = emergency** → sharp pain, shoulder pain, shock signs. Tx: methotrexate or surgery.

### Hydatidiform Mole (Molar Pregnancy)
- Grape-like vesicles, "snowstorm" on ultrasound, no FHTs
- Very high hCG, rapid uterine growth, early preeclampsia signs
- Follow hCG for **1 year** after evacuation (choriocarcinoma risk)

### Placenta Previa vs. Abruption

| | Previa | Abruption |
|---|-------|-----------|
| **Bleeding** | Painless, bright red | Painful, dark red |
| **Uterus** | Soft, relaxed | Firm, board-like |
| **Location** | Placenta covers cervix | Placenta separates from wall |
| **Assoc. with** | Prior C-sections | Cocaine, HTN, preeclampsia |

### Preeclampsia ⭐
**Triad**: Hypertension + Edema + Proteinuria (after 20 weeks)
- Risk factors: G1, obesity, age <19 or >35, multiples, chronic HTN, DM
- **HELLP** = Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets
- Tx: **Magnesium sulfate** (seizure prevention), antihypertensives (labetalol)
- **Cure = DELIVERY**

### Gestational Diabetes (GDM)
- Screen 24-28 weeks (glucose challenge)
- Risks: macrosomia, neonatal hypoglycemia, delayed lung maturation
- Tx: diet, exercise, insulin if needed
- True GDM → glucose normalizes by 6 weeks postpartum

### Rh Incompatibility
- Problem: **Rh− mom + Rh+ baby** → mom makes antibodies → destroys fetal RBCs (**erythroblastosis fetalis**)
- Prevention: **RhoGAM** at 28 weeks and within 72 hours after delivery

### TORCH Infections
| Letter | Infection | Key Points |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **T** | Toxoplasmosis | Cat feces, raw meat; neurologic damage |
| **O** | Other (syphilis, Hep B, GBS) | GBS: culture 35-37wk, IV abx in labor |
| **R** | Rubella | Cataracts, deafness, cardiac defects, IUGR; vaccine BEFORE pregnancy |
| **C** | CMV | Most common congenital viral infection |
| **H** | Herpes | C-section if active lesions at delivery |

### DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation)
- Clot formation AND clot destruction co-occur → depletes clotting factors
- Signs: bleeding from nose, gums, incisions, venipuncture sites; rising uterine size; tender hard uterus
- **Life-threatening emergency** → clotting factors + blood products

### Hypovolemic Shock Signs ⭐
- Fetal heart rate changes
- Rising weak pulse (**tachycardia = earliest sign**)
- Rising respiratory rate, shallow respirations
- **Falling BP** (late sign)
- ↓ Urine output (<30 mL/hr), pale/cold/clammy skin, faintness, thirst

### Anemias in Pregnancy
| Type | Key Facts |
|------|----------|
| **Iron-deficiency** | Most common; fatigue, pallor, tachycardia; Tx: oral iron x 3 months after correction |
| **Folic acid deficiency** | Tx: 1 mg/day (higher if prior NTD baby) |
| **Sickle cell** | Autosomal recessive; pregnancy can trigger crisis → vessel occlusion → preterm birth, IUGR; O2 + fluids throughout labor |

### Heart Disease in Pregnancy
- Blood volume ↑ 40-50% → can overwhelm compromised heart → **CHF**
- Increased clotting factors → **thrombosis risk**
- Fetus suffers from reduced placental blood flow

### UTIs in Pregnancy
- Increased UTI risk due to urinary stasis and hormonal changes
- **Pyelonephritis**: high fever, chills, flank pain, N/V → can trigger preterm labor
- Tx: antibiotics; encourage fluids, complete full course

### Harmful Substances in Pregnancy
Alcohol, cocaine, marijuana, tobacco, heroin, amphetamines, certain anticoagulants
